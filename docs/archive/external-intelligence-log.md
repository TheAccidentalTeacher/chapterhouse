# External Intelligence Log — Platforms, Tactics, and Internet Wisdom Worth Stealing Carefully

> *The internet is full of useful information, half-useful information, recycled information, and information wearing a fake mustache pretending to be wisdom. This document exists to sort the pile.*
>
> *— The Brand Whisperer*

---

## Purpose

This is the living intake log for outside ideas, platform guidance, marketing tactics, case studies, and "best practices" gathered from the web.

Its job is simple:
- keep useful information
- strip out hype
- translate platform advice into Next Chapter reality
- stop the brand from becoming a dumping ground for everybody else's opinions

---

## How to Use This Log

For every source or tactic, capture:

1. **Source** — who said it
2. **Category** — platform, tactic, workflow, trend, case study, operator insight
3. **Claim** — what they are actually saying
4. **Evidence level** — documented, plausible, anecdotal, fluffy, or suspect
5. **What is genuinely useful**
6. **What does not fit this brand**
7. **Decision** — adopt, test, watch, or ignore
8. **Next move** — what we actually do with it

---

## Evidence Scale

| Level | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **A — Verified** | Official docs, direct platform guidance, or clearly sourced evidence |
| **B — Strong Pattern** | Multiple credible sources saying similar things |
| **C — Plausible** | Makes sense, but needs testing or confirmation |
| **D — Anecdotal** | One operator's story; maybe useful, not doctrine |
| **F — Fluff** | Sounds good, says little, usually trying to sell something |

## Active Principle

**We are not trying to become HubSpot, Substack, or whoever shouted loudest on LinkedIn this week.**

We are trying to become more effective at being **us**.

That means outside information only matters when it sharpens:
- trust
- clarity
- discoverability
- revenue
- product quality
- customer experience

If it doesn't do one of those, it is probably just sophisticated procrastination.
