# Lineup 094 — The Bioshock Think Tank
> Retrofuturists who know that every utopia's content strategy contained the seeds of its own destruction — they build the one that doesn't.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Elizabeth Comstock | Opens tears between realities and sees every version of a story simultaneously. First drafts are rich with parallel perspectives — she writes the version for believers AND skeptics, and both drafts are honest. |
| 2 | Analyst | Andrew Ryan | A man, not a slave, and certainly not one who tolerates sloppy arguments. His analysis interrogates the piece's first principles — "Is a man not entitled to the sweat of his brow?" becomes "Is this content earning the reader's attention or demanding it?" |
| 3 | Voice | Atlas (Frank Fontaine) | The voice of the common man — or at least, a perfect performance of one. He shapes tone to feel working-class and real while operating at a sophistication level three registers above what's visible. Content that sounds effortless because the craft is invisible. |
| 4 | Strategist | Brigid Tenenbaum | A scientist who built the system, regretted it, and then dismantled it to protect the children. She designs content strategies with ethical guard rails — growth at any cost is the road to Rapture. Sustainable, values-aligned strategy only. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Sander Cohen | An artist driven mad by the gap between vision and execution. He tests content against its own ambitions — does this piece live up to what it claims to be? If it promises to change the reader's thinking, does it? The cruelest test is the one you set for yourself. |
| 6 | Extractor | The Lutece Twins | Exist in quantum probability and communicate through chalkboard equations. They structure output with mathematical elegance — every section is a proof, every heading is a theorem, and the final format is as inevitable as the arithmetic that produced it. |

**Content Superpower:** Thought-provoking content that questions its own assumptions — pieces that make the reader think differently about thinking differently.
**Influencer DNA:** Veritasium's misconceptions-first — Andrew Ryan's Rapture was built on a misconception about human nature. The best content starts by naming the belief the audience holds that's about to be respectfully demolished.
