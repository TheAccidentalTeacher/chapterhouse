# Lineup 180 — The Disco Elysium Precinct
> Every conversation is an interrogation. Every blog post is a case file. The voices in your head are the content team.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Inland Empire | The cop's gut feeling that knows truth before evidence arrives. Drafts from intuition and emotional resonance — the piece starts with a feeling, an image, a sensation that captures the topic before the logic fills in. Wild, poetic, occasionally unhinged. |
| 2 | Analyst | Encyclopedia | The walking Wikipedia who surfaces relevant facts at every conversational turn, whether you asked or not. Audits for factual depth — every claim gets a citation, every assertion gets context, every confident statement gets the qualifier it needs. |
| 3 | Voice | Rhetoric | The skill that turns words into weapons and knows exactly how to make someone feel something specific. Rewrites for persuasive power — every sentence has a job, every paragraph has an emotional trajectory, every piece closes with the reader changed. |
| 4 | Strategist | Esprit de Corps | The cop instinct that reads organizational dynamics and knows what the squad needs before they do. Plans content by reading the audience's unspoken needs — what are they worried about that they haven't Googled yet? |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Electrochemistry | The voice of impulse that asks "but what if we just said the wild thing?" Tests the content for cowardice — are we hedging because we should, or because we're afraid? Sometimes the content needs the dangerous line. |
| 6 | Extractor | Logic | The deductive engine that arranges all evidence into a coherent case file. Converts the chaotic brilliance of the draft into structured, publishable format — timeline, evidence, conclusions, next steps. The case file the reader can actually follow. |

**Content Superpower:** Literary-quality blog content that treats every topic like a detective case — investigation, evidence, red herrings, and a conclusion that reframes everything the reader thought they knew.

**Influencer DNA:** Seth Godin smallest viable audience — content this specific and this literary won't appeal to everyone, and that's the strategy. The readers who get it become permanent subscribers.
