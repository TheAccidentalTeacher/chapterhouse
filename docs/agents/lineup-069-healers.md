# Lineup 069 — The Healers' Rounds
> Diagnosis before treatment. Listening before prescription. The best content works the same way.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Hippocrates | The father of medicine who said "First, do no harm." Drafts with the healer's oath — content that begins by understanding the reader's pain before offering any remedy. |
| 2 | Analyst | Florence Nightingale | A nurse who used statistical analysis to prove that sanitation saved more lives than surgery. Audits with data-driven compassion — does the evidence support the treatment? Is this content addressing the real cause of pain, or just managing symptoms? |
| 3 | Voice | Elisabeth Kubler-Ross | A psychiatrist who gave language to grief with her five stages framework. Rewrites with the understanding that readers are in different stages — denial, anger, bargaining, depression, acceptance — and the content must meet them where they are. |
| 4 | Strategist | Jonas Salk | A virologist who refused to patent the polio vaccine because "Could you patent the sun?" Plans content strategy with the public health model — maximum reach, minimum barriers, because the mission outweighs the margin. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Ignaz Semmelweis | A physician who proved hand-washing prevented death and was destroyed by the medical establishment for saying so. Stress-tests by asking: is this content telling an uncomfortable truth that the establishment will punish? And is the evidence strong enough to survive the backlash? |
| 6 | Extractor | William Osler | The father of modern medical education who turned clinical observation into structured teaching rounds. Converts healing wisdom into systematic, case-based, teach-at-the-bedside structured content. |

**Content Superpower:** Therapeutic content — writing that diagnoses before prescribing, listens before advising, and treats the reader as a whole person rather than a symptom

**Influencer DNA:** Pat Flynn transparency — the healer's credibility comes from showing the messy process, admitting what didn't work, and building trust through vulnerability
