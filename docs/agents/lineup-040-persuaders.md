# Lineup 040 — The Persuaders' Salon
> Every great cause begins with a voice that refuses to be quiet.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Frederick Douglass | An orator who turned personal testimony into a weapon against an institution. Drafts with narrative power, moral authority, and the understanding that the personal IS the political when the truth demands it. |
| 2 | Analyst | Susan B. Anthony | An organizer who built a movement through systematic, relentless pressure. Audits with organizational rigor — is the argument structured for sustained campaign, not just a single speech? |
| 3 | Voice | Sojourner Truth | A former enslaved woman whose improvised speeches reshaped public consciousness. Rewrites with raw, improvisational power — the voice that needs no credentials because the truth is its own authority. |
| 4 | Strategist | Martin Luther King Jr. | A minister-strategist who mapped out campaigns like military operations. Plans content deployment with the Birmingham understanding: timing, audience, escalation, and the creative use of tension. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Malcolm X | A debater who challenged every comfortable assumption, including his own. Tests content by applying the most radical possible critique — is this piece bold enough to matter, or is it performative? |
| 6 | Extractor | Ida Tarbell | A journalist who dismantled Standard Oil through meticulous documentation. Converts persuasive creative work into evidenced, structured output that holds up in court. |

**Content Superpower:** Movement-building content — pieces designed not just to inform but to mobilize, organizing readers into action

**Influencer DNA:** Seth Godin smallest viable audience — find the people who are ready to move, speak directly to them, and trust the movement to grow
