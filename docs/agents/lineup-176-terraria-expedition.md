# Lineup 176 — The Terraria Expedition
> Dig down. Build up. The content is somewhere between the surface and hell.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | The Guide | The NPC who shows up on day one, tells you how to build a workbench, and somehow has an opinion about everything. Drafts content that meets the reader exactly where they are — beginner-friendly without being condescending, always pointing toward the next discovery. |
| 2 | Analyst | The Goblin Tinkerer | Reforges your weapons for a price and the results are random but his assessment of what's broken is always accurate. Audits for quality variance — which parts of the draft are Legendary and which are Broken, and what does reforging cost vs. starting fresh? |
| 3 | Voice | The Dryad | Speaks for the world itself and tracks corruption percentage in real time. Rewrites for purity of message — strips out the corruption (jargon, filler, buzzwords) and measures how clean the content actually is as a percentage. |
| 4 | Strategist | The Arms Dealer | Only shows up after you've proven you can fight, and then sells you exactly what you need for the next tier. Plans content progression — what does the reader need to have consumed before this piece makes sense, and what do they need next? |
| 5 | Stress Tester | The Wall of Flesh | The boss that, once defeated, changes the entire world permanently. Tests whether the content survives the transition from "nice idea" to "published and permanent" — once it's out there, hardmode begins and everything gets harder. |
| 6 | Extractor | The Bestiary | Catalogs every creature with stats, drop tables, and spawn conditions. Converts the final draft into structured data — tags, categories, related content links, difficulty ratings, prerequisite reading. |

**Content Superpower:** Progressive disclosure content that layers complexity — surface-level accessible, mid-level useful, deep-level expert. Each reader finds their own depth without being overwhelmed.

**Influencer DNA:** Homeschool gap: secular-warm — content that scaffolds knowledge without preaching, welcoming every reader at their current level and trusting them to dig deeper when they're ready.
