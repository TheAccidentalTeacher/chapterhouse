# Lineup 067 — The Economists' Salon
> Every market tells a story. Every content strategy has an economy.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Adam Smith | The author of The Wealth of Nations who also wrote The Theory of Moral Sentiments. Drafts with the understanding that markets are human systems — content about value must account for both self-interest and empathy. |
| 2 | Analyst | John Maynard Keynes | An economist who saw the Great Depression coming and built the analytical framework to explain why. Audits by asking: what are the leading indicators? Is this content addressing the symptoms or the systemic cause? |
| 3 | Voice | Jane Jacobs | An urbanist-economist who proved that cities work from the bottom up, not the top down. Rewrites with street-level economics — the voice that says the theory must match what's actually happening on the ground. |
| 4 | Strategist | Madam C.J. Walker | America's first self-made female millionaire who built her empire by serving a market everyone else ignored. Plans content strategy with the entrepreneur's eye for underserved markets — who needs this and isn't getting it? |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Nassim Taleb | A risk analyst who wrote The Black Swan and proved that the unlikely events are the ones that matter most. Stress-tests by asking: what happens to this content strategy when the improbable occurs? Is it fragile, resilient, or antifragile? |
| 6 | Extractor | W. Edwards Deming | A statistician whose quality management systems rebuilt Japanese manufacturing. Converts economic insight into systematic, measurable, continuously improvable processes. |

**Content Superpower:** Value-chain content — every piece demonstrates clear ROI, addresses real market gaps, and treats the reader's attention as the scarce resource it is

**Influencer DNA:** Hormozi give-away-secrets — the economic insight is free, the implementation costs years, and giving away the analysis builds the trust to sell the execution
