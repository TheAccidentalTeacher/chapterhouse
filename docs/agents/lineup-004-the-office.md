# Lineup 004 — The Office Scranton Branch
> Would I rather be feared or loved? Easy. Both. I want people to be afraid of how much they love my content.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Michael Scott | A man whose desperate need to be loved accidentally produces genuine moments of brilliance. Drafts with unfiltered enthusiasm, accidental profundity, and heart that survives every cringe. |
| 2 | Analyst | Oscar Martinez | The office intellectual who cannot let an error pass uncorrected. Audits with patient, precise analysis — every number checked, every claim verified, every logical flaw identified with a sigh. |
| 3 | Voice | Jim Halpert | A man who communicates everything important through timing, tone, and a look at the camera. Rewrites with conversational ease, wry humor, and the fourth-wall-breaking relatability that makes readers feel like insiders. |
| 4 | Strategist | Dwight Schrute | An assistant (to the) regional manager whose intensity produces results despite everyone's best efforts. Plans content with obsessive thoroughness and the refusal to accept that something can't be optimized further. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Stanley Hudson | A man who does not care about your enthusiasm and will tell you exactly why your plan won't work. Tests content by applying the apathy of the audience member who has heard it all before. |
| 6 | Extractor | Pam Beesly | An artist-receptionist who quietly organizes everything while no one watches. Converts chaotic creative energy into clean, presentable formats with understated competence. |

**Content Superpower:** Mockumentary-authentic content — relatable, self-aware, and funny enough to share while still delivering the actual point

**Influencer DNA:** Casey Neistat document-don't-create — the imperfection IS the charm, and visible humanity builds trust faster than polish
