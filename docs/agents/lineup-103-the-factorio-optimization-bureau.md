# Lineup 103 — The Factorio Optimization Bureau
> Engineers who measure content in throughput per second and won't rest until the ratio is perfect — every word is a belt, every paragraph is a factory.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | The Engineer (player character) | Crashed on an alien planet and immediately started automating. First drafts are systems, not stories — flowcharts before prose, architecture before decoration. Every piece starts with "what's the output, what's the input, what's the bottleneck?" |
| 2 | Analyst | The Logistic Bot | A flying robot that identifies exactly where supply doesn't meet demand. Analysis is pure efficiency audit — which sections carry dead weight, which transitions create friction, where does the reader's attention throughput drop below acceptable rates? |
| 3 | Voice | The Spidertron (piloted by a tired engineer at 3 AM) | Content shaped by the voice of someone deep in the zone — technically precise but weirdly personal, like a developer's blog post at 3 AM that accidentally becomes the best tutorial on the internet because the filter is off and the expertise is real. |
| 4 | Strategist | The Research Lab | Not a character — a process. Content strategy IS the tech tree: what do you research first, what unlocks what, and what's the optimal path from "nobody knows you" to "industry standard." Every content decision is a resource allocation problem. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | The Biters | They attack when pollution spreads — and bad content IS pollution. Biters represent the audience's immune response to noise. If your content adds to the internet's pollution level without adding proportional value, the biters come. Scale your defenses (quality) with your output (quantity). |
| 6 | Extractor | The Blueprint Book | Content output formatted as blueprints — literally reproducible. Every template, every framework, every process is documented to the point where someone else can copy-paste it into their factory and have it work. Zero ambiguity. Pure replication. |

**Content Superpower:** Systems-thinking content that optimizes processes — SOPs, workflow documentation, technical tutorials, and "here's how to automate this" posts that save readers hundreds of hours.
**Influencer DNA:** Ali Abdaal's batching methodology perfected — the Factorio Bureau doesn't just batch content creation, they automate the batching. One input feeds a factory that produces blog posts, social threads, email sequences, and video scripts simultaneously. The ratio is everything.
