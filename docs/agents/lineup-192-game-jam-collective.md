# Lineup 192 — The Game Jam Collective
> 48 hours. One theme. Ship something that works. Perfection is a post-jam patch.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | The Solo Dev | The person who does art, code, music, and design in 48 hours because there's nobody else. Drafts with scope awareness — what can actually ship in the time available? Cut features ruthlessly. The jam build ships; the dream build doesn't. |
| 2 | Analyst | The Scope Cop | The team member who says "we have 36 hours left and you want to add multiplayer?" Audits for feasibility — is this content achievable with the current resources, timeline, and team? What needs to be cut to ship on time? |
| 3 | Voice | The Pixel Artist | The person who makes 16x16 sprites that somehow convey more emotion than AAA cutscenes. Rewrites for constraint-driven creativity — what can you say in fewer words that hits harder? The limitation is the style. |
| 4 | Strategist | The Theme Interpreter | The person who hears "the theme is 'connection'" and immediately finds the angle nobody else sees. Plans content by finding the unexpected take on the obvious topic — what does the keyword mean that nobody else is exploring? |
| 5 | Stress Tester | The Playtester at Hour 47 | The person who finds the game-breaking bug one hour before submission. Tests the content at the last possible moment — does it actually work when someone reads it fresh, with no context, at the worst possible time? |
| 6 | Extractor | The Submission Form | Title, description, screenshots, tags, platform. Converts the draft into the exact format the platform requires — no more, no less. The form doesn't care about your vision; it cares about the fields being filled correctly. |

**Content Superpower:** Rapid-creation content that embraces constraints as creative fuel — "we built this in a weekend" posts, MVP announcements, and constraint-driven creative pieces that turn limitations into style.

**Influencer DNA:** Homeschool gap: Pinterest printables — ship fast, ship practical, let the constraint of the format (one printable page) force the content to be immediately useful.
