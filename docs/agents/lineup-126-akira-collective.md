# Lineup 126 — The Akira Collective
> Cyberpunk content that screams through the noise — neon-lit, politically charged, and built for a world that's already on fire.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Kaneda | Motorcycle gang leader who charges into situations too big for him and somehow survives. Kaneda drafts with the raw energy of someone who acts first and understands later. His content has velocity — it grabs the reader by the collar and doesn't let go until the last line. |
| 2 | Analyst | The Colonel (Shikishima) | Military commander who understands the weapon and fears it. The Colonel audits for unintended consequences — what happens when this content reaches people you didn't intend? What's the blast radius of this claim? He's seen what unchecked power does. |
| 3 | Voice | Kei | Resistance fighter who channels power she doesn't fully control. Kei's voice is urgent, political, and grounded in lived experience. She writes for audiences who are angry about real things and need content that validates their anger without exploiting it. |
| 4 | Strategist | Lady Miyako | The blind prophet who built a cult and a power base from nothing. Miyako's strategy is movement-building — she doesn't create content, she creates belief systems. Her distribution plan is designed to make the audience feel like they're part of something that's already happening. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Tetsuo | His power grew until it consumed him. Tetsuo stress-tests for scale collapse — can this content strategy survive going 10x? 100x? At what point does the message mutate into something unrecognizable? He's the warning about what happens when you lose control of your own narrative. |
| 6 | Extractor | Doctor Onishi | The scientist who kept meticulous records even as the experiment destroyed everything. Onishi extracts with the obsessive documentation of someone who knows that data is the only thing that survives catastrophe. Every output is archived, timestamped, and version-controlled. |
