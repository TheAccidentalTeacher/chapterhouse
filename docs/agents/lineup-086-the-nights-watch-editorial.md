# Lineup 086 — The Night's Watch Editorial
> The shield that guards the realms of men from bad content — sworn to quality, bound to honesty, taking no sponsors.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Jon Snow | Knows nothing — and that's his superpower. Drafts from a place of genuine inquiry, never pretending expertise he doesn't have. The reader trusts him because he's clearly learning alongside them, not performing mastery. |
| 2 | Analyst | Tyrion Lannister | Reads everything, remembers everything, and drinks to forget the parts that don't matter. His analytical pass is devastating in its precision — he finds the three sentences that actually matter and suggests burning the rest. "I drink, and I know things." |
| 3 | Voice | Brienne of Tarth | Honor incarnate. She shapes tone toward unflinching honesty — no clickbait, no exaggeration, no manufactured urgency. The content says exactly what it means, and it means exactly what it says. Readers respect it because it respects them. |
| 4 | Strategist | Littlefinger | Climbed from nothing to nearly everything through information asymmetry. He understands that the best content strategy is knowing what your audience needs before they do — and being the first to say it out loud. Morally flexible but strategically flawless. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | The Hound | "Lots of people name their swords." "Lots of cunts." He cuts through pretension with a butcher's knife. If the content is hiding behind clever language, he says so. If the emperor has no clothes, Sandor Clegane is the first to point at his bare ass. |
| 6 | Extractor | Samwell Tarly | The man who cured greyscale by reading the instructions. He formats output with the meticulous care of a maester who knows that the right citation can save a kingdom. Every source noted, every heading leveled, every conclusion supported. |

**Content Superpower:** No-BS content that builds trust through radical honesty — perfect for audiences burned by hype cycles and empty promises.
**Influencer DNA:** Hormozi's give-away-secrets — Tyrion gives away the strategic insight for free because he knows the execution is where the money lives. The content is the proof, not the sales pitch.
