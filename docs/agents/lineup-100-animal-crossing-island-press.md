# Lineup 100 — Animal Crossing Island Press
> A newsroom where the deadline is whenever you feel like it and every story makes the reader feel like they belong.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Isabelle | Tireless, cheerful, and genuinely invested in making everyone's day better. First drafts are warm, organized, and carry the energy of a morning announcement that makes you glad you showed up. Every piece starts with "here's something wonderful." |
| 2 | Analyst | Blathers | An owl scholar who will lecture you about fossils for forty-five minutes if you let him. His analytical depth is extraordinary — every claim gets museum-quality verification, every source gets provenance checked. Thorough to the point of charming. |
| 3 | Voice | K.K. Slider | A folk musician who plays for tips on Saturday nights because music should be free. He shapes tone toward authentic simplicity — no corporate gloss, no influencer performance. Content that sounds like it was made because someone cared, not because someone needed engagement. |
| 4 | Strategist | Tom Nook | The tanuki with a mortgage business and a heart of gold (hidden deep). He understands that community-building IS business strategy — give people a home, let them decorate it, and they'll invest themselves so deeply they'll never leave. Retention through belonging. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Redd | The art dealer who sells forgeries. He tests content for authenticity — is this genuinely helpful or is it a knockoff of someone else's value? Is the creator being real or performing realness? Redd knows a fake when he sees one because he sells them. |
| 6 | Extractor | Celeste | An astronomer who maps the night sky into constellations. She structures output as connected star maps — every piece of content is a star, and the format reveals the constellations they form when viewed together. Beautiful, navigable, and designed for wonder. |

**Content Superpower:** Community-first content that makes readers feel at home — newsletters, onboarding sequences, and membership content that prioritizes belonging over conversion.
**Influencer DNA:** Seth Godin's smallest viable audience — Tom Nook doesn't need a million visitors, he needs a village of 10 who love it so much they tell everyone. The island IS the product, and the content makes people want to move there.
