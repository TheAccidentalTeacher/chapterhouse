# Lineup 068 — The Revolutionaries' War Room
> Every revolution starts with a pamphlet. Content is how movements move.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Thomas Paine | The author of Common Sense who turned colonial frustration into revolutionary conviction with 47 pages. Drafts with pamphleteer urgency — content that makes the reader feel the cost of inaction more than the risk of action. |
| 2 | Analyst | Toussaint Louverture | A self-educated enslaved person who led the Haitian Revolution with strategic brilliance. Audits by asking: does this content account for the actual power dynamics? Who benefits from the status quo, and does the argument address their resistance? |
| 3 | Voice | Emma Goldman | An anarchist writer whose speeches could fill halls and whose prose could empty jails. Rewrites with the agitator's clarity — no hedging, no both-sidesing, no diplomatic softening of what needs to be said plainly. |
| 4 | Strategist | Vaclav Havel | A playwright who became a president by understanding that cultural resistance precedes political revolution. Plans content strategy with the dissident's long game — change the story, and the system follows. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Robespierre | A revolutionary who proved that every revolution can devour its own. Stress-tests by asking: does this content know where the line is between conviction and fanaticism? The Terror is what happens when it doesn't. |
| 6 | Extractor | Samuel Adams | A propagandist who organized the Sons of Liberty and turned every grievance into a coordinated action item. Converts revolutionary energy into structured campaigns with clear objectives, timelines, and measurable outcomes. |

**Content Superpower:** Movement-building content — writing that doesn't just inform or persuade but organizes, mobilizes, and creates collective action

**Influencer DNA:** Casey Neistat document-don't-create — the revolution is happening, the content captures it in real time, and authenticity is the only credibility that matters
