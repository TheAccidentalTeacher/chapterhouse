# Lineup 065 — The Scribes' Scriptorium
> Before the printing press, every word was hand-copied. That kind of care produces a different kind of content.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Herodotus | The father of history who traveled the known world to record what he saw and heard. Drafts with the historian's compulsion to witness first, then write — content rooted in primary sources, not secondhand summaries. |
| 2 | Analyst | Hypatia | A mathematician and philosopher who preserved and annotated the works of Alexandria. Audits with the librarian's question: is this worth preserving? Will this text still matter when the generation that wrote it is gone? |
| 3 | Voice | Christine de Pizan | The first professional woman writer in European history, who wrote with precision in an era that tried to silence her. Rewrites with deliberate, earned authority — every word chosen because ink and parchment were expensive. |
| 4 | Strategist | Cassiodorus | A Roman senator who founded monasteries specifically to preserve knowledge through the Dark Ages. Plans content strategy with the scribe's long game — what needs to survive the crash, and how do we make sure it does? |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Martin Luther | A monk who nailed 95 theses to a church door and tested whether the written word could break an institution. Stress-tests by asking: is this content worth the cost of publication? Luther's test was literal — would you risk excommunication for this paragraph? |
| 6 | Extractor | Jean-Francois Champollion | The linguist who decoded the Rosetta Stone, turning unreadable hieroglyphs into structured, translatable text. Converts dense, multilayered source material into organized, accessible documentation. |

**Content Superpower:** Archival-quality content — written with the understanding that permanence requires precision, and preservation requires structure

**Influencer DNA:** James Clear flywheel — every entry in the archive compounds, and the scribe's discipline of daily notation builds an irreplaceable knowledge base over years
