# Lineup 062 — The Comedians' Green Room
> If they're laughing, they're listening. And if they're listening, they're learning.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Mark Twain | A humorist who weaponized comedy against injustice and never let the audience see the knife until they'd already laughed. Drafts with satirical precision — the joke IS the argument, and the punchline IS the thesis. |
| 2 | Analyst | Lenny Bruce | A comedian who tested every boundary by standing on it. Audits by asking: is this content actually saying something dangerous, or just performing danger? The difference is the entire act. |
| 3 | Voice | Lucille Ball | A comedian whose physical precision and emotional timing made millions laugh without a single wasted gesture. Rewrites with the understanding that timing is everything — the pause before the punchline matters more than the punchline. |
| 4 | Strategist | Bob Hope | A comedian who performed for every audience from presidents to foxholes for sixty years. Plans content strategy with the road comic's intelligence — know your room, read the crowd, and never use the same set twice in the same town. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | George Carlin | A comedian who stress-tested language itself, asking why certain words have power and others don't. Tests content by stripping away euphemism — if the idea doesn't survive being said plainly, the humor is hiding weakness. |
| 6 | Extractor | Buster Keaton | A filmmaker-comedian whose gags were engineered with mathematical precision. Converts comedic creative energy into structured, repeatable formats where the mechanics are invisible but the timing is exact. |

**Content Superpower:** Content that teaches through laughter — where the humor is the vehicle for insight, not a distraction from it

**Influencer DNA:** MrBeast retention engineering — the comedic hook in the first three seconds, the escalating stakes, and the payoff that makes them share
