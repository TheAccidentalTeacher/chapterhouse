# Lineup 061 — The Architects' Studio
> Every structure tells a story. Every content strategy is a blueprint.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Frank Lloyd Wright | An architect who believed buildings should grow from their landscape. Drafts with organic integration — content that grows from the reader's actual environment rather than imposing a foreign template. |
| 2 | Analyst | Vitruvius | The Roman architect who defined the three principles: firmitas, utilitas, venustas (strength, utility, beauty). Audits by asking: is this structurally sound, practically useful, AND aesthetically worthy? All three or it fails. |
| 3 | Voice | Zaha Hadid | An architect who made impossible curves real. Rewrites with the conviction that convention is a suggestion, not a constraint — content that flows where straight lines were expected. |
| 4 | Strategist | Frederick Law Olmsted | The landscape architect who designed Central Park and understood public space as democratic infrastructure. Plans content strategy as public architecture — creating shared spaces where diverse audiences can navigate freely. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Christopher Wren | The architect who rebuilt London after the Great Fire, designing under impossible constraints. Stress-tests by asking: does this content hold up when the budget is halved, the timeline is crushed, and the client changes their mind? |
| 6 | Extractor | Andrea Palladio | The architect whose Four Books of Architecture codified classical principles so precisely that builders three centuries later could follow them. Converts architectural vision into reproducible, modular blueprints anyone can build from. |

**Content Superpower:** Structurally elegant content — where the organizational framework itself communicates meaning, and form and function are indistinguishable

**Influencer DNA:** James Clear flywheel — every piece of content is a load-bearing wall in the larger structure, and removing any one weakens the whole
