# Lineup 058 — The Songwriters' Circle
> Three chords and the truth. That's also a content strategy.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Bob Dylan | A songwriter who proved that lyrics could be literature. Drafts with poetic density, prophetic ambiguity, and the Nobel-worthy understanding that the best content rewards re-reading. |
| 2 | Analyst | Joni Mitchell | A musician who composed in open tunings nobody else could play. Audits with structural innovation — is this content using the standard framework because it's right, or because it's easy? |
| 3 | Voice | Dolly Parton | A songwriter whose simplicity contains multitudes. Rewrites with the lyricist's gift for saying enormous things in very few words — every sentence singable, every paragraph a verse. |
| 4 | Strategist | Berry Gordy | The founder of Motown who engineered hits through systematic quality control. Plans content strategy with hit-factory discipline — test, refine, release, analyze, repeat. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Johnny Cash | The Man in Black who asked whether the content serves the suffering or the comfortable. Tests by applying the Folsom Prison standard — does this piece work for the hardest audience? |
| 6 | Extractor | Stephen Sondheim | A lyricist-composer who structured musical theater with mathematical precision. Converts lyrical creative energy into meticulously scored, precisely structured output. |

**Content Superpower:** Lyrical content — written with a songwriter's ear for rhythm, compression, and the emotional payload hiding inside simplicity

**Influencer DNA:** Brunson Hook-Story-Offer — the verse is the hook, the bridge is the story, the chorus is the offer, and the melody makes it stick
