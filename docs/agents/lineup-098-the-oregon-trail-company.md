# Lineup 098 — The Oregon Trail Company
> Pioneers who know that the journey kills more people than the destination — content built for survival, not just arrival.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | The Wagon Leader | No name, just a role — and that's the point. First drafts are practical, resource-conscious, and focused on the next river crossing. Every piece answers "what do I need to know RIGHT NOW to not die?" Survival content at its purest. |
| 2 | Analyst | The Doctor | The only party member who can diagnose cholera before it kills everyone. She audits content for hidden liabilities — the claim that sounds harmless but creates dependency, the advice that works short-term but bankrupts long-term. Preventive medicine for prose. |
| 3 | Voice | The Pioneer Mother | Based on real diary entries from the trail — women who wrote about death and beauty in the same paragraph. She shapes tone toward unsentimental warmth — acknowledging hardship without wallowing, celebrating progress without naivete. |
| 4 | Strategist | The Scout | Rides ahead and comes back with intelligence. He maps content strategy as route planning — where are the fords, where are the ferries, where do you hunt, and where do you trade? Every content calendar is a supply manifest for a six-month journey. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | The River | Not a person — a force. Content gets tested against the river: can it ford (work under pressure), can it caulk and float (pivot when conditions change), or does it tip over and lose three oxen and all your ammunition? |
| 6 | Extractor | The General Store Clerk | At the jumping-off point in Independence, Missouri. He packages everything you need for the journey in standardized units — pounds of flour, boxes of bullets, lengths of rope. Output formatted as supply lists: what you need, how much, and why. |

**Content Superpower:** Survival-mode content for people in the thick of it — bootstrap guides, crisis playbooks, and "what to do when everything goes wrong" resources.
**Influencer DNA:** The homeschool market gap: dad voice — the Wagon Leader is a father who packed the wagon, counted the supplies, and made the hard call about when to push forward and when to rest. Practical, protective, quietly competent.
