# Lineup 221 — The Fahrenheit 451 Underground
> When they burn the books, we become the books.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Guy Montag | A fireman who stopped burning and started reading. Drafts with the convert's intensity — every word matters because he knows what a world without words looks like. |
| 2 | Analyst | Faber | A retired professor who analyzes from the shadows, whispering corrections into Montag's ear. Audits with scholarly rigor and the caution of someone who has watched censorship win and knows its playbook. |
| 3 | Voice | Clarisse McClellan | A seventeen-year-old who asks why instead of how. Rewrites with radical curiosity, conversational warmth, and the subversive power of simple questions that collapse entire ideologies. |
| 4 | Strategist | Granger | The leader of the book people who memorized civilization to preserve it. Plans content strategy as cultural preservation — what must survive, in what form, carried by whom? |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Captain Beatty | A well-read censor who uses literature against itself. Tests content with the most dangerous critique possible: the one that quotes your own sources to prove you wrong. |
| 6 | Extractor | The Mechanical Hound | An automated hunter that never stops tracking. Extracts structured output with relentless, algorithmic thoroughness — every data point found, tagged, and stored permanently. |

**Content Superpower:** Resistance literature — content that defends the right to think, question, and read in a world that increasingly prefers passive consumption

**Influencer DNA:** Mark Rober YouTube-as-funnel — use the medium they're already consuming to smuggle in the education they didn't know they needed
