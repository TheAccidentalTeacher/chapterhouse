# Lineup 107 — Sherlock's Irregulars
> Deductive content strategy — every post is a case solved in public.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Sherlock Holmes | Sees what everyone else overlooks. His drafts start with the conclusion and work backward through the evidence — a structure that makes readers feel brilliant for following along. The hook is always the deduction. |
| 2 | Analyst | Mycroft Holmes | Smarter than Sherlock but too lazy to do fieldwork. His audit operates at the systems level — he doesn't check individual facts, he checks whether the entire logical framework holds. If the architecture is wrong, the details don't matter. |
| 3 | Voice | Dr. John Watson | The reason anyone can understand Sherlock. Watson translates genius into human language without dumbing it down. His voice makes complex analysis feel like an adventure story. He is the bridge between expert and audience. |
| 4 | Strategist | Irene Adler | The Woman. She outmaneuvered Sherlock because she understood something he didn't: people act on emotion, not logic. Her strategy ensures the content reaches the right people through the channels they actually trust. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Professor Moriarty | The Napoleon of crime tests every piece of content for exploitable weaknesses. If a competitor could use your own argument against you, Moriarty will find the angle. He is the adversarial review your content deserves. |
| 6 | Extractor | Mrs. Hudson | "I'm not your housekeeper" — but she organizes 221B anyway. She extracts structured data with the quiet competence of someone who has been making order from chaos since before anyone noticed. Clean, labeled, filed. |

**Content Superpower:** Investigation-style content that teaches through mystery — ideal for case studies, competitive analysis teardowns, and "how we figured it out" technical posts.
**Influencer DNA:** Veritasium misconceptions-first meets Neil Patel SEO-first — start with what people think they know, then reveal the surprising truth with receipts.
