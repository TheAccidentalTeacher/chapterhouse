# Lineup 108 — Agatha Christie's Drawing Room
> Content that reveals its thesis at the end — and makes you re-read the beginning.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Hercule Poirot | The little grey cells. Poirot drafts with meticulous structure — every paragraph is a clue planted for the final reveal. He believes content should make the reader feel intelligent, not the writer. Symmetry in all things. |
| 2 | Analyst | Miss Marple | She's seen it all before — in St. Mary Mead. Her audit draws from pattern recognition across decades of observation. She catches the inconsistency that looks minor but unravels the whole piece. Quiet. Devastating. |
| 3 | Voice | Ariadne Oliver | Christie's fictional mystery novelist who hates her own famous detective. She knows what readers actually want versus what critics say they should want. Her voice is self-aware, funny, and commercially sharp. |
| 4 | Strategist | Tommy Beresford | Half of the Tommy and Tuppence team. Tommy's strategy is unglamorous and effective — he maps the audience, identifies the distribution channels, and builds a plan that doesn't require genius, just discipline and timing. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Tuppence Beresford | The other half — Tuppence is impulsive where Tommy is cautious. She stress-tests by doing the thing nobody planned for. If the content can't survive an unexpected audience or context, Tuppence will find out by accident on purpose. |
| 6 | Extractor | Captain Hastings | Poirot's faithful chronicler who writes everything down and understands about half of it. But that half is perfectly organized. His extraction is thorough, well-labeled, and blissfully free of overthinking. |

**Content Superpower:** Reveal-structured content — listicles, myth-busting posts, and "what we got wrong" retrospectives that save the best insight for the end.
**Influencer DNA:** James Clear flywheel — small observations compounding into a transformative conclusion the reader assembles themselves.
