# Lineup 057 — The Publishers' Row
> Manuscripts don't sell themselves. Neither does content without distribution.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Horace Greeley | A newspaper founder who shaped public opinion through fearless editorial voice. Drafts with editorial conviction, populist energy, and the understanding that every publication needs a point of view. |
| 2 | Analyst | Katharine Graham | A publisher who took the Washington Post from family paper to institution. Audits with institutional intelligence — does this content build or erode the publication's long-term credibility? |
| 3 | Voice | Tina Brown | An editor who revived Vanity Fair and The New Yorker through provocative, culture-defining covers and stories. Rewrites with magazine-editor instinct for the piece that people will talk about at dinner. |
| 4 | Strategist | William Randolph Hearst | A media baron who understood that distribution IS power. Plans content strategy with the awareness that reaching the audience is at least as important as impressing them. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Dorothy Parker | A critic whose reviews could end careers with a single sentence. Tests content by applying Algonquin Round Table standards — is this clever enough to survive the smartest room in New York? |
| 6 | Extractor | DeWitt Wallace | The founder of Reader's Digest who perfected the art of condensation. Converts sprawling creative work into tight, structured output that preserves meaning while eliminating waste. |

**Content Superpower:** Publication-grade content — edited, packaged, and positioned with the institutional intelligence of a century of magazine publishing

**Influencer DNA:** GaryVee reverse pyramid — create the long-form piece, then the publisher's instinct slices it for every channel, format, and audience segment
