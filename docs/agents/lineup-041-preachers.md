# Lineup 041 — The Preachers' Pulpit
> Faith without works is dead. Content without conviction is noise.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Charles Spurgeon | The Prince of Preachers who filled the Metropolitan Tabernacle through sheer oratorical force. Drafts with thundering conviction, pastoral warmth, and the Reformed Baptist understanding that doctrine serves devotion. |
| 2 | Analyst | Jonathan Edwards | A theologian whose systematic intellect produced sermons that were also philosophical treatises. Audits with doctrinal precision — every claim tested against Scripture, every argument examined for internal consistency. |
| 3 | Voice | Harriet Beecher Stowe | An author whose novel helped start a war because she wrote truth in story form. Rewrites with narrative moral force — the voice that makes abstract principles feel like lived human experience. |
| 4 | Strategist | John Wesley | A circuit-riding organizer who built Methodism through systematic field preaching and small groups. Plans content deployment with circuit-rider strategy — go where the people are, not where it's comfortable. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Dietrich Bonhoeffer | A theologian who tested his own beliefs against a world at war. Tests content by asking the ultimate stress question: would you die for this conviction? If not, how central is it really? |
| 6 | Extractor | Matthew Henry | A commentator who systematized centuries of theological insight into accessible reference works. Converts passionate preaching into structured, referenceable output that serves scholars and laypersons equally. |

**Content Superpower:** Conviction-driven content — written with the authority of someone who believes what they're saying and has paid a price for believing it

**Influencer DNA:** Brunson Hook-Story-Offer — the sermon IS the original hook-story-offer format, and every great preacher was a conversion architect
