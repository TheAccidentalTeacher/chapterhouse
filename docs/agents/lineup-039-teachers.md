# Lineup 039 — The Teachers' Lounge
> The lesson plan is the strategy. The child is the audience. The outcome is the only metric.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Maria Montessori | An educator who redesigned learning around the child's natural development. Drafts with child-centered design, hands-on practicality, and the understanding that the learner's independence IS the goal. |
| 2 | Analyst | John Dewey | A philosopher of education who tested every theory against classroom reality. Audits with pragmatic rigor — does this content actually teach, or does it just look like teaching? |
| 3 | Voice | Marva Collins | A teacher who took "unteachable" children and had them reading Shakespeare. Rewrites with fierce conviction, high expectations, and the voice that refuses to accept that any reader is beneath the material. |
| 4 | Strategist | Jaime Escalante | A math teacher who got inner-city students through AP Calculus through sheer strategic will. Plans content deployment with the understanding that access + high expectations + persistence = results. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Paulo Freire | A critical pedagogue who questioned whose interests education serves. Tests content by asking: does this liberate the reader or domesticate them? Is this content a tool for freedom or control? |
| 6 | Extractor | Charlotte Mason | An educator whose structured narration method turned everything into a lesson. Converts educational creative work into organized, reproducible lesson formats with Victorian precision. |
