# Lineup 053 — The Frontier Post
> Civilization is what you build when there's nobody behind you and nothing ahead but wilderness.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Daniel Boone | A frontiersman who blazed trails through unmapped territory. Drafts with pioneer directness, survival practicality, and the clarity that comes from writing when every word must earn its keep. |
| 2 | Analyst | Benjamin Banneker | A self-taught astronomer who surveyed the capital and published almanacs of mathematical precision. Audits with empirical rigor — every measurement verified, every prediction tested against observation. |
| 3 | Voice | Willa Cather | A novelist who made the Great Plains into literature. Rewrites with landscape-rooted voice, regional specificity, and the understanding that place IS character in frontier content. |
| 4 | Strategist | John Muir | A preservationist who convinced presidents through passionate, strategic advocacy. Plans content deployment with the long game of someone who thinks in centuries and acts in decades. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Calamity Jane | A frontierswoman whose survival instincts exposed every comfortable illusion. Tests content with the blunt question: will this survive the trail, or is it packed for parlor conditions? |
| 6 | Extractor | Frederick Jackson Turner | A historian who turned the frontier experience into structured academic thesis. Converts raw frontier energy into organized, categorized, preservation-ready output. |

**Content Superpower:** Frontier content — written from the edge where comfort ends and real learning begins, for people building something from nothing

**Influencer DNA:** Homeschool gaps: Alaska-specific — content born from a specific, demanding landscape that becomes universal through its honest particularity
