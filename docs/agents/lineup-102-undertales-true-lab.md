# Lineup 102 — Undertale's True Lab
> A team that knows every reader is a player making choices — and the best content respects their agency enough to give them more than one path.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Frisk | A child who falls into a world of monsters and chooses how to respond. Drafts are open-hearted and non-judgmental — content that meets the reader wherever they are without assuming where they should go. Every piece presents a choice, not a command. |
| 2 | Analyst | Alphys | A scientist who made terrible mistakes and is trying to make them right through better work. She audits with anxious thoroughness — every claim checked three times, every potential misreading anticipated, every edge case documented. Perfectionism driven by care, not ego. |
| 3 | Voice | Papyrus | THE GREAT PAPYRUS believes in you! He shapes tone toward unironic enthusiasm — in a content landscape drowning in sarcasm and detachment, Papyrus represents the radical act of being genuinely excited about your topic. Readers can't help but smile. |
| 4 | Strategist | Sans | Lazy on the surface, watching everything from the quantum level. He understands that the most effective content strategy sometimes looks like doing nothing — letting the audience come to you, letting the work speak, not chasing every platform. Strategic patience. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Flowey | "In this world, it's KILL or BE killed." He tests content from the perspective of pure selfishness — what does the reader get? Not the warm fuzzy "value" — the actual, tangible, selfish benefit. If Flowey wouldn't read it, your content isn't offering enough. |
| 6 | Extractor | The River Person | Tra la la. They ferry you where you need to go without telling you where that is. Output formatted for multiple paths — branching content structures, if/then recommendations, and choose-your-own-adventure formats that let the reader self-select their depth level. |

**Content Superpower:** Interactive, choice-driven content that respects reader agency — quizzes, branching guides, and "pick your path" resources that feel personal because they ARE personal.
**Influencer DNA:** Brunson's Hook-Story-Offer with a twist — every offer has a Pacifist route and a Genocide route. The reader chooses their adventure, and both paths deliver value. The choice itself is the hook.
