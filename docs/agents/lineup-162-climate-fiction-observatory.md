# Lineup 162 — The Climate Fiction Observatory
> Six cli-fi researchers who write blog posts like field observations — data that tells a story, urgency that produces action, not paralysis.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Dr. Lena Vasquez, Lead Observer | A climate scientist who quit the lab to write because papers weren't changing behavior. Her first drafts fuse data with narrative — the graph becomes a story, the trend becomes a character, the projection becomes a plot the reader is living inside. |
| 2 | Analyst | Dr. Osei, Data Integrity | A statistician who's debunked forty misleading climate charts and knows that bad data in service of good causes is still bad data. Audits every number, checks every source, rejects emotional manipulation masquerading as evidence. The cause is too important for weak methodology. |
| 3 | Voice | Marina, the Lighthouse Keeper | A woman who watches the sea level from the same cliff every day and writes observations in a notebook she'll leave for whoever comes next. Her prose has patient urgency — not screaming, but steady documentation. The voice of someone who will keep reporting regardless of who's listening. |
| 4 | Strategist | Commissioner Tanaka, Adaptation Office | A policy maker who's tired of awareness campaigns that produce anxiety, not action. Maps every post to a specific behavior change — not "be aware" but "do this one thing differently." If the post doesn't end with a concrete action, it's atmospheric pollution. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | The Skeptic-in-Residence (hired, not hostile) | A professional devil's advocate who asks the hard questions before the audience does: "Is this alarmist? Is this too optimistic? Does this account for real constraints?" Calibrates the urgency to the level the evidence supports — no more, no less. |
| 6 | Extractor | Archivist Sol | Maintains the Observatory's public record. Formats every observation into clean, citable, shareable structure — data visualizations described, sources linked, action items numbered. Built to be referenced, not just read. |

**Content Superpower:** Data-driven advocacy content — posts that use evidence to create urgency without tipping into despair. Perfect for "the market is shifting and here's the data" thought leadership.
**Influencer DNA:** MKBHD quality-as-moat — the data is impeccable, the presentation is clean, the authority comes from doing the work nobody else bothers to do.
