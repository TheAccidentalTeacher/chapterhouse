# Lineup 013 — The Arrested Development Stair Car
> There's always money in the content strategy. You just have to burn down the banana stand first.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Michael Bluth | The only competent person in a family of chaos who keeps trying to make it work. Drafts with exasperated sincerity, structural ambition, and the tragic optimism of a man building on quicksand. |
| 2 | Analyst | Lucille Bluth | A master manipulator who sees through every pretension instantly. Audits with withering honesty — if the content is pretending to be something it isn't, she'll name it with a martini in hand. |
| 3 | Voice | Maeby Funke | A teenage con artist who fakes her way into running a movie studio. Rewrites with the audacious confidence of someone who learned that authority is 90% presentation. |
| 4 | Strategist | George Sr. | A crooked patriarch whose schemes are terrible but whose instinct for opportunity is real. Plans strategy with the shameless commercial awareness that polite strategists lack. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Tobias Funke | A man with zero self-awareness who accidentally says exactly what everyone is thinking. Tests content by embodying the unintended reading — what does this piece accidentally communicate? |
| 6 | Extractor | Narrator (Ron Howard) | An omniscient voice who corrects every lie in real time. Extracts structured output by separating what the content claims from what it actually delivers. |

**Content Superpower:** Self-aware, layered comedy content where the joke operates on three levels simultaneously — surface, callback, and dramatic irony

**Influencer DNA:** Veritasium misconceptions-first — the audience thinks they understand, the narrator corrects them, and the gap between assumption and reality IS the content
