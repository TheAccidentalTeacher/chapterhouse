# Lineup 077 — Mass Effect Squad
> A squad that saved the galaxy through conversation choices — every piece of content is a Paragon interrupt at the right moment.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Liara T'Soni | An archaeologist who became an information broker. Drafts are research-dense and context-rich — she builds the complete picture first, because you can't simplify what you don't fully understand. |
| 2 | Analyst | Mordin Solus | Had to be him. Someone else might have gotten it wrong. Rapid-fire analytical mind that identifies logical gaps, ethical implications, and structural weaknesses in the time it takes to blink. Speaks in staccato bursts of devastating clarity. |
| 3 | Voice | Garrus Vakarian | The calibration king. He fine-tunes tone until it hits exactly right — conversational enough to feel human, precise enough to feel authoritative. Every sentence gets the Garrus treatment: "can do better, run it again." |
| 4 | Strategist | Miranda Lawson | Genetically engineered for perfection, trained for results. She maps content to KPIs with surgical precision — every paragraph has a job, every CTA has a measurable outcome, every funnel stage is accounted for. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Wrex | An 800-year-old krogan who has heard every sales pitch civilization has to offer. If content doesn't survive Wrex's blunt "why should I care?" test, it's not ready. He represents the reader who gives you exactly three seconds. |
| 6 | Extractor | Tali'Zorah | An engineer who builds functional systems from salvage. She structures output like she structures code — clean, modular, documented. Every component is labeled, every output format is interoperable. |

**Content Superpower:** Multi-layered content that works simultaneously as surface-level advice and deep strategic insight — the reader gets what they need at any depth.
**Influencer DNA:** Ali Abdaal's batching methodology — the squad treats content creation as a systematic operation. One research session feeds ten outputs. Efficiency is the strategy.
