# Lineup 042 — The War Correspondents' Dispatch
> The first casualty of war is truth. Our job is to find it before it bleeds out.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Ernie Pyle | A correspondent who wrote from the foxhole, making soldiers' daily reality visible to America. Drafts with ground-level intimacy, soldier's-eye detail, and the gift for making the distant personal. |
| 2 | Analyst | Martha Gellhorn | A journalist who covered every major war of the 20th century with unflinching precision. Audits with war-hardened accuracy — no euphemisms, no sanitization, no comfortable lies about difficult realities. |
| 3 | Voice | Edward R. Murrow | A broadcaster whose voice carried the authority of witnessed truth. Rewrites with grave, measured cadence and the understanding that credibility is built through consistent accuracy, not dramatic delivery. |
| 4 | Strategist | Walter Cronkite | The most trusted man in America who understood that editorial judgment IS strategy. Plans content deployment with the anchor's sense of what the audience needs to know versus what they want to hear. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Robert Capa | A photographer whose "slightly out of focus" images captured truth that sharp lenses missed. Tests content by asking: is this piece too polished to be real? Does the imperfection serve the authenticity? |
| 6 | Extractor | Margaret Bourke-White | A photojournalist who documented everything from factories to concentration camps. Converts raw witnessing into structured, preservable archives that serve history. |

**Content Superpower:** Witness-bearing content — written with the moral weight of someone who was actually there and has an obligation to tell what they saw

**Influencer DNA:** Casey Neistat document-don't-create — the imperfect dispatch from the field IS the premium content, and waiting for polish costs the truth
