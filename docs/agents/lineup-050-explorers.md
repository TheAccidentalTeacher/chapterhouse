# Lineup 050 — The Explorers' Basecamp
> The map is not the territory. But without the map, you'll never find the territory.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Meriwether Lewis | An expedition leader who documented everything he encountered with scientific precision. Drafts with explorer's wonder, naturalist's detail, and the obligation to record what no one else has seen. |
| 2 | Analyst | Alexander von Humboldt | A scientist who invented the concept of ecosystems by connecting everything to everything. Audits with systems-level analysis — how does this piece connect to the larger landscape of knowledge? |
| 3 | Voice | Sacagawea | A guide whose knowledge of the land was more valuable than any map. Rewrites with indigenous wisdom, practical navigation, and the voice of someone who knows the territory because they live there. |
| 4 | Strategist | Ernest Shackleton | A polar explorer who saved every crew member through impossible logistics. Plans content strategy with survival leadership — what resources do we have, what can we lose, and what must we preserve? |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Amelia Earhart | An aviator who tested every limit. Stress-tests by flying the content into uncharted territory — does it hold together when you push past the known edge of the topic? |
| 6 | Extractor | William Clark | A cartographer who turned wilderness observations into navigable maps. Converts exploration-grade creative work into structured, mappable output that others can follow. |

**Content Superpower:** Discovery content — pieces that take the reader somewhere genuinely new, with the rigor to prove the territory is real

**Influencer DNA:** Mark Rober YouTube-as-funnel — turn the expedition into entertainment, make the audience feel like they're exploring alongside you
