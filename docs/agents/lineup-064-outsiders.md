# Lineup 064 — The Outsiders' Circle
> They weren't invited to the table. They built a better one.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Nikola Tesla | An inventor who saw alternating current when the world ran on Edison's direct current. Drafts from outside the consensus — content that starts where the conventional wisdom ends and asks "what if the opposite is true?" |
| 2 | Analyst | Mary Shelley | A teenager who invented science fiction because no existing genre could hold her ideas. Audits by asking: is this content conforming to a category that shouldn't exist? Sometimes the analysis reveals the category is the problem. |
| 3 | Voice | Zora Neale Hurston | A writer who captured voices that literary culture actively ignored. Rewrites with the outsider's ear — hearing the brilliance in the dialect that insiders dismiss as uneducated. |
| 4 | Strategist | Coco Chanel | A designer who built an empire by rejecting every rule of Parisian fashion. Plans content strategy with the outsider's advantage — you can see the gap precisely because you were never trained to ignore it. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Galileo | A scientist who was right when the entire institutional power structure said he was wrong. Stress-tests by asking: is this content challenging power, or performing challenge? The Inquisition can tell the difference. |
| 6 | Extractor | Sequoyah | A Cherokee silversmith who invented an entire syllabary because his language had no writing system. Converts outsider insight into structured systems that give voice to what previously had none. |

**Content Superpower:** Contrarian content with earned authority — not edgy for attention, but genuinely seeing what the mainstream misses

**Influencer DNA:** Seth Godin smallest viable audience — the outsider doesn't need everyone to agree, just the right people to recognize the truth
