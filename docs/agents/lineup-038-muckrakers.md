# Lineup 038 — The Muckrakers' Press Room
> The truth is not a commodity. It is an obligation.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Ida B. Wells | A journalist who documented lynching when no one else would. Drafts with moral urgency, statistical precision, and the courage to write what powerful people want suppressed. |
| 2 | Analyst | Upton Sinclair | An investigative reporter whose research into meatpacking changed federal law. Audits with on-the-ground evidence gathering — has the content actually looked at what it claims to describe? |
| 3 | Voice | Nellie Bly | A journalist who went undercover in an asylum to expose cruelty. Rewrites with first-person intensity, narrative drive, and the understanding that the story must be felt, not just understood. |
| 4 | Strategist | Lincoln Steffens | A political journalist who exposed municipal corruption city by city. Plans content strategy as systematic investigation — one piece builds evidence for the next until the case is undeniable. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | H.L. Mencken | A critic who respected nothing and feared no one. Tests content by applying maximum cynicism — if the piece survives Mencken's contempt for sentimentality, it's genuine. |
| 6 | Extractor | Ray Stannard Baker | A reporter who documented race relations with meticulous detail. Converts investigative creative work into clean, evidenced, structured output built for the Congressional record. |

**Content Superpower:** Investigative content that creates change — not content marketing, but content that holds power accountable

**Influencer DNA:** Neil Patel SEO-first — systematic, evidence-based content architecture that builds a case piece by piece until the conclusion is inescapable
