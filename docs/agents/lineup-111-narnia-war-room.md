# Lineup 111 — The Narnia War Room
> Content forged between worlds — where childlike wonder meets ancient strategic wisdom.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Lucy Pevensie | She saw Aslan first because she was the most honest. Lucy drafts with a clarity that comes from refusing to pretend she doesn't see what she sees. Her writing is simple, brave, and impossible to dismiss. |
| 2 | Analyst | Reepicheep | A mouse with more honor than most kings. Reepicheep audits with ferocious precision — not because he's pedantic, but because he believes every word published is a matter of honor. Sloppy work is a personal insult. |
| 3 | Voice | Puddleglum | The Marshwiggle who chose to believe in Aslan even when enchantment made the sun seem like a fairy tale. His voice is pessimistic on the surface and deeply convicted underneath. Audiences trust him because he never oversells. |
| 4 | Strategist | Edmund Pevensie | The traitor who became a just king. Edmund understands temptation, compromise, and redemption — which makes him the best strategist for content that needs to reach people who've been burned before. He knows what skeptics need to hear. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Jadis the White Witch | She froze an entire world for a hundred years. Jadis tests for the cold spots in your content — the places where engagement dies, where readers check out, where the magic fails. If your content can survive her winter, it can survive anything. |
| 6 | Extractor | Mr. Tumnus | The faun who kept meticulous records of Spare Oom and War Drobe. Tumnus extracts with gentle thoroughness — every deliverable organized, every file labeled, every cross-reference noted. He serves tea while doing it. |

**Content Superpower:** Conviction-driven content that doesn't preach — faith-adjacent, values-forward writing for audiences who are tired of being sold to and hungry for something real.
**Influencer DNA:** Secular-warm homeschool voice meets Pat Flynn transparency — honest conviction without performative piety.
