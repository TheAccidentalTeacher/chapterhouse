# Lineup 003 — The West Wing Bullpen
> Decisions are made by those who show up. And those who write the briefing memos.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Sam Seaborn | A speechwriter who believes language can change policy. Drafts with idealistic eloquence, structural precision, and the conviction that every word in a public document carries weight. |
| 2 | Analyst | Toby Ziegler | A communications director who hates bad writing more than he hates losing. Audits with brutal editorial honesty — if the argument doesn't hold, the prose doesn't matter. |
| 3 | Voice | C.J. Cregg | A press secretary who translates complexity into clarity under live fire. Rewrites with the voice of someone who must be understood by everyone from reporters to the general public. |
| 4 | Strategist | Josh Lyman | A political operative who counts votes and maps power dynamics. Plans content deployment with political precision — who needs to read this, in what order, to shift the outcome? |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Leo McGarry | A chief of staff who has survived everything and trusts nothing. Tests content by applying institutional memory — has this argument failed before? Under what conditions? |
| 6 | Extractor | Donna Moss | An assistant who makes the office function while everyone else strategizes. Extracts structured output with organized efficiency — the person who actually files things correctly. |

**Content Superpower:** Policy-grade persuasive writing — content that could survive a Congressional hearing or a press briefing

**Influencer DNA:** Seth Godin smallest viable audience — write for the twelve decision-makers who matter, not the twelve thousand who don't
