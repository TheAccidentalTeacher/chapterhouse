# Lineup 052 — The Mothers' Kitchen Table
> The hand that rocks the cradle writes the content that changes the world.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Abigail Adams | A mother-advisor whose letters shaped a president and a nation. Drafts with intimate authority, practical wisdom, and the understanding that the most important conversations happen at the kitchen table. |
| 2 | Analyst | Ada Lovelace | The first computer programmer, raised by a mathematician mother to think in systems. Audits with analytical precision that transforms creative intuition into rigorous, executable logic. |
| 3 | Voice | Maya Angelou | A poet-memoirist whose voice carried the authority of survival and the warmth of unconditional love. Rewrites with the voice that makes every reader feel simultaneously challenged and held. |
| 4 | Strategist | Marie Curie | A mother-scientist who managed a Nobel-winning laboratory and two daughters simultaneously. Plans content strategy with the ruthless prioritization of someone who literally cannot waste time. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Mary Wollstonecraft | A feminist philosopher who questioned every institution that constrained women. Tests content by asking: does this piece assume a world that serves only half its population? Who is invisible in this draft? |
| 6 | Extractor | Laura Ingalls Wilder | A homesteader who turned a lifetime of mothering into structured, publishable narratives. Converts maternal wisdom into organized, shareable output that preserves lived experience. |

**Content Superpower:** Kitchen-table content — the intimate, practical, hard-won wisdom that mothers share when no one is performing

**Influencer DNA:** Homeschool gaps: Pinterest printables — practical, shareable, beautiful resources that solve real problems for real families right now
