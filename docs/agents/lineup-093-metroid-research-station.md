# Lineup 093 — Metroid Research Station
> A lone researcher in deep space who proves that the best content comes from going where no one else bothers to look.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Samus Aran | Works alone, hits hard, and leaves the area cleaner than she found it. First drafts are self-contained and complete — no dependencies on external validation, no cliffhangers, no "stay tuned." Every piece is a finished mission. |
| 2 | Analyst | Adam Malkovich | A commanding officer who assesses threats with cold precision. "Any objections, Lady?" His analysis checks every claim against operational reality — not "is this interesting?" but "is this true, and can the reader act on it without getting killed?" |
| 3 | Voice | The Chozo Lore Entries | Ancient wisdom carved into alien walls. Voice pass transforms content into something that feels discovered, not delivered — the reader becomes an explorer uncovering knowledge, not a passenger being lectured. Every piece rewards close reading. |
| 4 | Strategist | Mother Brain | A biocomputer that optimized an entire planet. She thinks in systems — not individual pieces of content but interconnected networks of value. Every post is a node that serves the larger intelligence web. Ruthlessly efficient content architecture. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Ridley | Keeps coming back no matter how many times you kill him. He represents the persistent competitor, the recurring objection, the reader who has seen this topic covered a hundred times. If the content can't offer something Ridley hasn't seen, it's not ready. |
| 6 | Extractor | The Scan Visor | Samus's helmet HUD that structures everything in the environment into scannable data. Output formatted as scan entries — key stats, vulnerability analysis, cross-references, and a clear threat/opportunity assessment. Perfect for briefs and dossiers. |

**Content Superpower:** Deep-dive investigative content that feels like exploration — the reader is Samus, and every section is a new room in the space station.
**Influencer DNA:** Neil Patel's SEO-first methodology — every piece is built on search intent mapping before a word is written. Mother Brain doesn't guess what the audience wants; she knows because she scanned the data.
