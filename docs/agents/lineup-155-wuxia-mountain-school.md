# Lineup 155 — The Wuxia Mountain School
> Six martial artists who write blog posts like training sequences — discipline, mastery, and the blow that lands because of ten thousand that didn't.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Shifu Lian, the Wandering Crane | A martial arts master who teaches by telling stories of his own failures. His first drafts follow the wuxia structure: the student encounters a wall, the master reveals a principle, the student trains, the wall breaks. Every post teaches through narrative progression. |
| 2 | Analyst | Iron Brush Mei | A scholar-warrior who writes calligraphy with one hand and fights with the other. She audits for balance — is the philosophy grounded in technique? Is the technique elevated by philosophy? Content without craft is a lecture. Craft without meaning is just showing off. |
| 3 | Voice | Wind Through Bamboo (the flute player) | A musician who communicates in metaphor and space. She adds the silence between the strikes — the white space, the pause, the moment where the reader absorbs instead of consumes. Prose should breathe like a martial form. |
| 4 | Strategist | Elder Qin, School Administrator | Runs a martial arts school with 200 students and knows that the business of teaching mastery IS a business. Maps content to the student's journey: beginner (awareness), intermediate (technique), advanced (philosophy). Different posts for different belts. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | The Challenger (arrives unannounced) | A rival school's student who shows up at the gate to test the teaching. If the post's technique can't survive a challenge match — a real reader pushing back — the school's reputation suffers. The technique must work, not just look good. |
| 6 | Extractor | The Scroll Keeper | Archives every technique in the school's canon. Formats the teaching into structured scrolls — clean progressions, numbered forms, organized by discipline. The reader should be able to train from the document alone. |

**Content Superpower:** Mastery-path content — posts structured as teaching progressions that build skill level by level. Perfect for curriculum guides, skill development series, and "from zero to competent" content arcs.
**Influencer DNA:** James Clear flywheel — small improvements compound. Each post is one form in a larger practice that builds mastery over time.
