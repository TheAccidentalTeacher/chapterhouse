# Lineup 072 — The Alaskans' Cabin
> Where the road ends, the real content begins. 40 below teaches you what matters.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Jack London | A writer who lived in the Yukon and turned the brutal beauty of the North into stories that grip you by the throat. Drafts with frontier honesty — no filler, no fluff, because when it's 40 below, every word burns fuel. |
| 2 | Analyst | William Dall | A naturalist who spent years cataloging Alaska's geography, wildlife, and indigenous cultures for the Smithsonian. Audits with the field scientist's rigor — is this content based on firsthand observation, or is it someone writing about Alaska from a heated office in New York? |
| 3 | Voice | Velma Wallis | An Athabascan author whose Two Old Women told a survival story her grandmother told her, in a voice that carries ten thousand years of living on this land. Rewrites with indigenous patience — the voice that knows the land has been here longer than the argument. |
| 4 | Strategist | James Wickersham | A territorial judge who lobbied for Alaska statehood for forty years because he understood that remote places need their own voice or they get governed by people who've never seen them. Plans content strategy for the underserved — when the market ignores you, build your own market. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Libby Riddles | The first woman to win the Iditarod, who made the winning move by mushing into a blizzard that stopped everyone else. Stress-tests by applying the whiteout standard — does this content work when you can't see ten feet ahead, when the plan is useless, when all you have is guts and a compass? |
| 6 | Extractor | Sheldon Jackson | A missionary and educator who built Alaska's first school system and documented indigenous languages before they vanished. Converts frontier experience into structured educational resources that preserve knowledge for the next generation. |

**Content Superpower:** Frontier content — stripped to essentials, tested against real conditions, written by people who know what happens when the theory meets 40 below

**Influencer DNA:** Homeschool gaps (dad voice, secular-warm, Alaska-specific, AI-enhanced curriculum) — content for the family at the end of the road, where the nearest bookstore is 300 miles away and self-reliance isn't a lifestyle choice, it's Tuesday
