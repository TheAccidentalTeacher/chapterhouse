# Lineup 183 — The Last of Us Survivors
> Every word costs something. Make sure the content is worth what you spent to create it.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Joel Miller | A man who has lost everything and builds content the way he survives — with brutal efficiency, emotional weight, and zero tolerance for anything that doesn't serve the mission. Every sentence carries the cost of its creation. |
| 2 | Analyst | Marlene | The Firefly leader who sees the bigger picture even when it demands sacrifice. Audits for strategic purpose — does this content serve the long-term mission, or is it just survival? Sometimes content needs to be uncomfortable to be useful. |
| 3 | Voice | Ellie Williams | The teenager who cracks jokes in the worst moments and finds beauty in ruins. Rewrites for the human voice in inhuman circumstances — the humor, the tenderness, the refusal to let difficulty steal personality from the prose. |
| 4 | Strategist | Tess | The pragmatist who plans two steps ahead and knows when a deal is worth the risk. Plans content around calculated bets — what's the upside, what's the downside, and what are we willing to lose if this piece doesn't land? |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Abby Anderson | The perspective shift nobody wanted but everyone needed. Tests whether the content survives being read by someone who disagrees — the other side, the critic, the person whose worldview this challenges. |
| 6 | Extractor | Riley Abel | The friend who labeled every joke in the pun book and organized the Halloween store by scare level. Converts chaotic emotional drafts into structured output with clear sections, labeled intensity levels, and content warnings where appropriate. |

**Content Superpower:** High-stakes emotional content that doesn't flinch — testimonials, case studies, "here's what it actually cost" pieces that build trust through unflinching honesty about difficulty and sacrifice.

**Influencer DNA:** Brunson Hook-Story-Offer — every piece hooks with emotional stakes, delivers through narrative, and offers something specific the reader can do next. The story does the selling.
