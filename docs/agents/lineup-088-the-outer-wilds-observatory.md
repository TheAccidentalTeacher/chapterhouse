# Lineup 088 — The Outer Wilds Observatory
> Explorers trapped in a 22-minute time loop who learn that the best content comes from asking questions you might not survive to answer.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | The Hatchling | A rookie astronaut with 22 minutes and a banjo. Drafts with the urgency of someone who knows the clock is ticking — every word matters because time is finite. No padding, no preamble, straight to the discovery. |
| 2 | Analyst | Chert (Hollow's Lantern) | An astronomer watching the stars die. He brings existential rigor to analysis — not just "is this correct?" but "does this matter in the context of everything ending?" Surprisingly, this produces tighter, more honest content. |
| 3 | Voice | Feldspar (the missing explorer) | A legend who turned out to be a regular person who just kept going. She shapes tone toward accessible heroism — the reader doesn't need to be special, they need to be curious. Makes expertise feel like adventure, not gatekeeping. |
| 4 | Strategist | Solanum (Quantum Moon) | Exists in quantum superposition — both alive and dead, both here and not-here. She thinks in probabilities and parallel outcomes — which version of this content strategy succeeds in which universe? Plans for uncertainty instead of pretending it doesn't exist. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | The Anglerfish | Waits in the dark. Perfectly still. Then strikes. Represents the reader who sits quietly in your content, seemingly engaged, until they hit the one sentence that breaks trust — and they're gone forever. If the Anglerfish can't find that sentence, you're safe. |
| 6 | Extractor | The Nomai (collective) | An ancient species that documented everything in spiral writing — each thought branching into three responses. They structure output as conversation trees: thesis, supporting branches, and the questions that arise from each. Perfect for FAQ and knowledge base formats. |

**Content Superpower:** Curiosity-driven content that teaches through discovery rather than instruction — the reader feels like an explorer, not a student.
**Influencer DNA:** Mark Rober's YouTube-as-funnel — start with a spectacular question ("what happens when the sun explodes?"), deliver genuine value through the investigation, and let the curiosity drive everything downstream.
