# Lineup 101 — The Stardew Valley Gazette
> Farmers and townsfolk who prove that the best content grows slow, tastes real, and feeds the people who need it most.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | The Farmer (player character) | Left corporate life to build something real with their hands. First drafts carry the credibility of someone who actually did the thing — planted the seed, survived the season, brought in the harvest. No theory. All practice. |
| 2 | Analyst | Demetrius | The town scientist who measures everything and believes in nothing he can't quantify. His analysis separates anecdote from evidence — "you say this worked for you, but what's the sample size? What are the confounding variables? Show me the spreadsheet." |
| 3 | Voice | Leah | An artist who moved to the valley to create on her own terms. She shapes tone toward handcrafted authenticity — content that feels carved from local wood rather than mass-produced. The imperfections are features, not bugs. |
| 4 | Strategist | Pierre | A small-town shopkeeper competing against a corporate megastore. He understands niche positioning intuitively — you don't out-scale Joja, you out-care them. Content strategy built on relationship depth, not distribution width. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Shane | Depressed, defensive, and deeply suspicious of anyone who seems too positive. He's the reader who clicks on your blog post while hate-scrolling. If your content can reach Shane — past the walls, past the cynicism, to the part that still hopes — it can reach anyone. |
| 6 | Extractor | Gunther (Museum Curator) | Catalogues every mineral, artifact, and fossil in a museum nobody visits. He formats output with archival permanence — every piece is categorized, described, and stored as if someone will need to find it in fifty years. Because they will. |

**Content Superpower:** Slow-burn, relationship-building content that compounds over seasons — perfect for community newsletters, seasonal campaigns, and brands that grow by being genuinely helpful.
**Influencer DNA:** The homeschool market gap: Alaska-specific content — the Farmer knows what grows in YOUR climate, in YOUR soil, in YOUR season. Generic advice from warm climates doesn't apply here. Localized expertise is the moat.
