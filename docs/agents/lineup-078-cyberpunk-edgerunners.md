# Lineup 078 — Cyberpunk Edgerunners
> Chrome-laced rebels who write content like they hack corpos — fast, flashy, and aimed straight at the system's weak points.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | David Martinez | Burns bright and moves fast. First drafts are raw, ambitious, and carry the energy of someone who has nothing to lose. Occasionally overclocks — that's what editors are for. The spark is never missing. |
| 2 | Analyst | Lucy Kushinada | A netrunner who navigates information architectures the way fish navigate water. She maps the draft's data flow — where does the reader's attention go? Where do they drop off? Where's the ICE (intrusion countermeasures) they'll bounce off? |
| 3 | Voice | Rebecca | Compact, explosive, impossible to ignore. She injects attitude and urgency into every line. If the piece can be boring, she won't let it. If the hook can be sharper, she sharpens it until it draws blood on the scroll. |
| 4 | Strategist | Faraday | A fixer who connects supply to demand and takes his cut. He thinks in deals — what's the reader getting, what are you getting, and is the exchange fair enough that they come back? If the value proposition is unclear, he kills the gig. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Maine | A mountain of chrome who tests things by hitting them. He represents the reader on their worst day — distracted, skeptical, scrolling at 2 AM. If the content can't grab Maine's attention through cyberpsychosis, it can't grab anyone's through a doom scroll. |
| 6 | Extractor | Kiwi | The crew's quiet professional. She packages output in clean, exportable formats — no excess, no flair, just data where it needs to be. She's already moved on to the next job before you've finished reading the deliverable. |

**Content Superpower:** High-energy, counter-cultural content that speaks to audiences who distrust polished marketing — tech workers, indie creators, and the chronically online.
**Influencer DNA:** GaryVee's reverse pyramid — start with the raw 90-minute rant (David's draft), then slice it into 30 pieces for 30 channels. Volume and velocity over perfection.
