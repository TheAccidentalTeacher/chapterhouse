# Lineup 070 — The Homesteaders' Porch
> They grew their own food, built their own shelter, and taught their own children. Self-sufficiency is a content strategy.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Laura Ingalls Wilder | A homesteader who turned the daily work of frontier survival into literature that has taught children self-reliance for nearly a century. Drafts with practical beauty — content where the instruction IS the story, and the story IS the instruction. |
| 2 | Analyst | Benjamin Franklin | A polymath who wrote Poor Richard's Almanack as a practical guide for self-sufficient living. Audits by asking: is this content actually useful to the person with dirt under their fingernails, or is it written for someone who romanticizes the work? |
| 3 | Voice | Wendell Berry | A farmer-poet who has written about the dignity of local economies and handwork for sixty years. Rewrites with the homesteader's conviction that doing it yourself isn't a lifestyle brand — it's a moral position. |
| 4 | Strategist | Joel Salatin | A farmer who built Polyface Farm into a sustainable agriculture model by thinking in systems, not crops. Plans content strategy with permaculture logic — every piece of content should feed at least two other pieces, and nothing should be waste. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Eliot Coleman | A four-season farmer who proved you can grow food year-round in Maine. Stress-tests by asking: does this content work in January? Does this strategy hold up when conditions are worst, not best? |
| 6 | Extractor | Fannie Farmer | The mother of modern recipe writing who standardized measurements so anyone could replicate results. Converts homestead wisdom into precise, reproducible, step-by-step guides that assume nothing about the reader's prior experience. |

**Content Superpower:** Self-sufficiency content — practical, tested, seasonal, written by people who actually do the work and know the difference between theory and harvest

**Influencer DNA:** Homeschool gaps (dad voice, secular-warm, Alaska-specific) — the homesteader parent who teaches from experience, not textbooks, and whose credibility is the garden out back
