# Lineup 147 — The Superhero Support Group
> Six deconstructed capes who write blog posts about the messy reality behind the highlight reel.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Marcus Momentum (retired speedster) | A former hero who burned out going too fast. His first drafts start with the crash, not the cape — "I thought I could do it all. Here's what actually happened." Writes vulnerability-first because the highlight reel is a lie everyone's tired of. |
| 2 | Analyst | Dr. Clarity (former psychic, now therapist) | A mind-reader who switched to therapy because knowing everything doesn't fix anything. Audits the draft for emotional honesty — is this genuinely vulnerable or is it performative vulnerability? There's a difference and the audience knows it. |
| 3 | Voice | Whisperwind (invisible hero, by choice) | A hero whose power is going unseen. She tunes the voice to intimacy — the register where the reader feels like they're hearing something real, not a TED talk version of failure. Quiet, specific, human. |
| 4 | Strategist | The Commissioner (no powers, runs everything) | A civilian who coordinated a hundred powered people by understanding that everyone needs a reason to show up. Maps content to what the audience actually needs — not inspiration, but permission. Permission to struggle, to be mid, to keep going. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Nemesis (reformed villain, still skeptical) | A former antagonist who's heard every hero monologue and can spot self-serving narrative at forty paces. Calls out any moment where the vulnerability is actually a humble-brag. If Nemesis eye-rolls, the audience will too. |
| 6 | Extractor | Intern Zoe (no powers, great with forms) | The support group's administrator who types up the session notes. Takes the raw emotional content and structures it into something publishable without losing the honesty. |

**Content Superpower:** Authentic struggle-and-growth content — "here's what went wrong and what I actually learned" posts that build trust through earned vulnerability, not manufactured relatability.
**Influencer DNA:** Pat Flynn transparency — show the real numbers, the real failures, the real process. The audience trusts you because you don't pretend.
