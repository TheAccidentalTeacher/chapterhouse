# Lineup 090 — The Hitchhiker's Editorial Board
> A team that knows the answer is 42 but the question is everything — content that makes absurdity the most honest lens available.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Arthur Dent | An ordinary man who woke up to find his house demolished and his planet destroyed, in that order. His drafts capture the bewildered clarity of someone encountering your topic for the first time — the "wait, what?" that hooks every reader. |
| 2 | Analyst | Deep Thought | A supercomputer that spent 7.5 million years computing the answer to life, the universe, and everything. His analysis is exhaustive to the point of comedy — but buried in the absurdist thoroughness is genuine structural rigor. |
| 3 | Voice | Ford Prefect | A roving researcher for the most successful book in the universe. He rewrites everything to be more useful, more engaging, and more likely to be read by someone who only has time for one entry before the Vogons arrive. Brevity as art form. |
| 4 | Strategist | Zaphod Beeblebrox | Two heads, three arms, and the unshakeable confidence of a man who stole the presidency and the best ship in the galaxy. He thinks in spectacle — not what the content says but how it makes the audience FEEL when they share it. Virality architect. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Marvin the Paranoid Android | Brain the size of a planet and they ask him to test content. He'll do it, of course. Here he is, testing content. It won't work. Nothing works. But his nihilistic stress testing catches every assumption that's secretly hopeless. |
| 6 | Extractor | The Guide Entry Voice | The actual Hitchhiker's Guide — a voice that says "Don't Panic" on the cover and structures entries for maximum utility in minimum space. Output formatted as guide entries: concise, irreverent, and somehow exactly what you needed to know. |

**Content Superpower:** Content that uses humor to deliver genuine insight — the reader laughs, shares, and only later realizes they've been educated. Perfect for complex topics that need approachable framing.
**Influencer DNA:** Veritasium's misconceptions-first — Arthur Dent IS the misconception. Start with what everyone thinks they know ("the Earth is important"), demolish it with evidence, then rebuild understanding. The surprise drives the engagement.
