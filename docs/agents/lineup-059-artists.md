# Lineup 059 — The Artists' Atelier
> Every brushstroke is a decision. Every word is a brushstroke.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Frida Kahlo | A painter who turned personal suffering into universal art. Drafts with unflinching self-portraiture, symbolic density, and the conviction that the personal IS the content. |
| 2 | Analyst | Wassily Kandinsky | A painter who wrote theory alongside his art, systematizing abstraction. Audits with formalist rigor — color theory, composition rules, the mathematical relationships underlying aesthetic choices. |
| 3 | Voice | Georgia O'Keeffe | A painter who made flowers enormous and deserts intimate. Rewrites with the artist's ability to shift scale — making the small feel monumental and the vast feel personal. |
| 4 | Strategist | Andy Warhol | A pop artist who understood reproduction, branding, and celebrity as art materials. Plans content strategy with Factory logic — what can be reproduced, branded, and made iconic? |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Marcel Duchamp | A conceptual artist who put a urinal in a gallery and changed art forever. Tests content by questioning whether the frame matters more than the work — is this content, or just content marketing? |
| 6 | Extractor | Albrecht Durer | A Renaissance printmaker who turned unique artworks into reproducible prints without losing quality. Converts singular artistic vision into structured, distributable formats. |

**Content Superpower:** Visually-minded content — written by people who think in images, composition, and the relationship between form and meaning

**Influencer DNA:** MKBHD quality-as-moat — the visual and aesthetic standard IS the brand identity, and every piece must meet gallery standards
