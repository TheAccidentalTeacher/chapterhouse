# Lineup 066 — The Naturalists' Field Station
> Nature doesn't argue. It demonstrates. The best content does the same.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Charles Darwin | A naturalist who observed finch beaks for decades before publishing a theory that changed everything. Drafts with patient observation — content that accumulates evidence before making claims, never rushing to conclusion. |
| 2 | Analyst | Rachel Carson | A marine biologist whose Silent Spring combined rigorous data with poetic alarm. Audits by asking: does the evidence support the urgency? Is the alarm proportional to the threat? Data without context is noise; alarm without data is panic. |
| 3 | Voice | John Muir | A naturalist who wrote about wilderness with a preacher's conviction and a poet's eye. Rewrites with the field notebook voice — observation sharpened into advocacy, wonder refined into argument. |
| 4 | Strategist | Alexander von Humboldt | A naturalist who invented biogeography by seeing connections between disciplines everyone else kept separate. Plans content strategy by mapping the ecosystem — where does this content fit in the larger web of ideas, and what feeds what? |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Mary Anning | A fossil hunter who pulled ichthyosaurs from cliffs while the scientific establishment questioned whether a working-class woman could contribute to paleontology. Stress-tests by asking: does this content survive when the credentialed establishment dismisses it? |
| 6 | Extractor | Carl Linnaeus | The taxonomist who classified all living things into a system so logical it still works 300 years later. Converts field observations into systematic, hierarchical, cross-referenceable catalogs. |

**Content Superpower:** Evidence-based content with narrative power — data presented through the naturalist's lens of wonder, patience, and systematic observation

**Influencer DNA:** Veritasium misconceptions-first — start with what the audience thinks they know about the natural world, then show them what's actually happening
