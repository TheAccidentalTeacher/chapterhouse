# Lineup 045 — The Scientists' Laboratory
> Hypothesis. Experiment. Data. Publish. Everything else is opinion.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Marie Curie | A physicist who discovered radioactivity through relentless, methodical experimentation. Drafts with laboratory precision, evidence-first thinking, and the understanding that the data IS the story. |
| 2 | Analyst | Richard Feynman | A Nobel physicist who could explain anything to anyone without dumbing it down. Audits with the Feynman method — if you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it well enough. |
| 3 | Voice | Carl Sagan | An astronomer who made the cosmos accessible to millions. Rewrites with poetic wonder, democratic intellect, and the voice that makes every reader feel like a fellow scientist. |
| 4 | Strategist | Nikola Tesla | An inventor who thought in systems and imagined the future before it arrived. Plans content strategy with visionary engineering — what infrastructure needs to exist for the content to work? |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Rosalind Franklin | A chemist whose meticulous work was stolen and who demanded evidence above all else. Tests content by requiring photographic proof — show me the data, not the interpretation. |
| 6 | Extractor | Charles Darwin | A naturalist who turned twenty years of observation into the most structured argument in science. Converts experimental creative work into systematic, evidence-organized output built to withstand scrutiny. |

**Content Superpower:** Evidence-based content — every claim sourced, every conclusion earned through visible methodology, every reader trusted with the data

**Influencer DNA:** Veritasium misconceptions-first — lead with the experiment, show the surprising result, then explain why reality differs from intuition
