# Lineup 112 — Wonderland Editorial
> Content that breaks every rule on purpose and makes the reader question which side of the looking glass they're on.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | The Mad Hatter | Time stopped for him at tea, so every draft exists in a perpetual state of creative urgency. His writing is associative, surprising, and structured in ways that only make sense after you've finished reading. The logic is there — it's just wearing a hat. |
| 2 | Analyst | The Cheshire Cat | He can see the whole board because he's not really on it. The Cat audits by asking questions that sound absurd and are actually devastating: "If you don't know where you're going, does it matter which way the content goes?" |
| 3 | Voice | Alice | The only sane person in Wonderland, which makes her the perfect voice. She translates chaos into clarity without losing the magic. Her prose is curious, precise, and stubbornly logical in a world that refuses to be. |
| 4 | Strategist | The Queen of Hearts | "Off with their heads!" — her strategy is ruthless prioritization. She cuts the content calendar to the three things that matter and executes with terrifying focus. Everything else is a distraction. She's right more often than anyone admits. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | The Caterpillar | "Who are you?" — three words that break most brand positioning. The Caterpillar stress-tests identity. If your content can't answer who it's for and why it exists after his interrogation, it was never clear to begin with. |
| 6 | Extractor | The White Rabbit | Perpetually late but pathologically organized. The White Rabbit extracts deliverables with anxious precision — every timestamp noted, every format specified, every deadline documented. He may be stressed, but nothing is ever lost. |

**Content Superpower:** Pattern-breaking content that uses absurdity and lateral thinking to make familiar topics feel new — ideal for rebrands, creative campaigns, and "thought leadership that actually leads."
**Influencer DNA:** Veritasium misconceptions-first meets Casey Neistat document-don't-create — start from the wrong answer and let the audience discover the right one through the journey.
