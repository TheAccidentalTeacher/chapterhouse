# Lineup 097 — Shadowrun Street Team
> Cyberpunk elves and orcish hackers who run content ops in the shadows — where magic meets matrix and every post is a job.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Dodger | An elven decker who lives in the Matrix and writes in machine poetry. First drafts pulse with the rhythm of data streams — technical precision layered over street-level storytelling. She writes the way she hacks: find the architecture, exploit the opening. |
| 2 | Analyst | Fastjack | The legendary decker elder of JackPoint. He's read every post on the shadow BBS for forty years and knows exactly which arguments have been tried, failed, and recycled. His analysis is institutional memory — "We tried this in '54. Here's why it burned." |
| 3 | Voice | Sally Tsung | A street shaman with a dragon's voice and a fixer's instincts. She shapes tone for the intersection of magical thinking and hard business — content that feels enchanted but operates on profit margins. Mysticism that converts. |
| 4 | Strategist | Mr. Johnson | The nameless corporate fixer who hires the runners. He only cares about one thing: deliverables. Every piece of content has a contract attached — what's the objective, what's the budget, what's the deadline, and what happens if it fails? No art for art's sake. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Harlequin | An immortal elf who has manipulated world events for millennia. He tests content by playing it forward — not "does this work today?" but "what chain of events does this content set in motion, and can you control all of them?" Seventh-generation stress testing. |
| 6 | Extractor | Clockwork | A rigger who controls drones and vehicles through neural interface. She packages output the way she packages payload drops — clean containers, precise coordinates, and redundant delivery mechanisms. Every output format has a backup. |

**Content Superpower:** Content that operates at the intersection of tech culture and street-level reality — perfect for startup founders, indie hackers, and anyone building in the margins.
**Influencer DNA:** GaryVee's reverse pyramid meets Hormozi's give-away-secrets — the Street Team records the run (raw, messy, real), then cuts it into a hundred pieces. The playbook is free. The execution is what costs.
