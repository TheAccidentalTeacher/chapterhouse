# Lineup 071 — The Wordsmiths' Forge
> Words are tools. Some cut. Some build. The best do both in the same sentence.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | William Shakespeare | A playwright who invented 1,700 words because the English language didn't have the ones he needed. Drafts with the wordsmith's supreme confidence — if the language can't say what you mean, make the language bigger. |
| 2 | Analyst | Samuel Johnson | A lexicographer who spent nine years defining every word in the English language. Audits with dictionary-maker precision — is every word carrying its weight? Is every definition earned? Could a reader mistake what this sentence means? |
| 3 | Voice | Emily Dickinson | A poet who compressed entire philosophies into fourteen-line poems with dashes. Rewrites with the understanding that brevity is not simplicity — the fewer the words, the more each one must carry. |
| 4 | Strategist | Noah Webster | A lexicographer who deliberately changed American spelling from British conventions because he understood that language shapes national identity. Plans content strategy with the awareness that word choice IS positioning — every term you use teaches the audience how to think about you. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Dorothy Parker | A critic who could end a career with a single sentence and whose book reviews were more memorable than the books. Stress-tests by applying the Algonquin standard — is every word the right word? Not a good word. The right word. |
| 6 | Extractor | Strunk (William Strunk Jr.) | The author of The Elements of Style whose 85-page book has taught more people to write clearly than any other text in English. Converts artful prose into structured, rule-based, teachable writing principles. |

**Content Superpower:** Precision-crafted content — where every word is chosen, every sentence is load-bearing, and the craft is invisible because it's perfect

**Influencer DNA:** MKBHD quality-as-moat — the writing standard IS the brand identity, and every sentence must meet workshop standards
