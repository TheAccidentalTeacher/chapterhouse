# Lineup 154 — The Rom-Com Writers' Room
> Six romantic comedy writers who know that the best content makes you laugh, then feel something, then act.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Nora McAllister, Showrunner | A TV writer who's run three hit rom-coms and knows that every great episode starts with a misunderstanding. Her first drafts open with the meet-cute between the reader and the problem — awkward, relatable, and immediately engaging because everyone's been there. |
| 2 | Analyst | David Chen, Continuity Editor | The person who makes sure the love interest's eyes don't change color between episodes. Audits for internal consistency, logical progression, and the kind of plot holes that make smart audiences throw popcorn. |
| 3 | Voice | Priya Sharma, Dialogue Polish | A dialogue specialist who knows that the best lines sound like they weren't written. Tunes every sentence until it feels like a conversation — warm, witty, human. The opposite of corporate content voice. |
| 4 | Strategist | Max Ortega, Network Notes | The executive who approves the season order. Maps every episode (post) to the season arc (content calendar) — is this a premiere, a mid-season pivot, or a finale? Every piece of content has a position in the relationship between brand and audience. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Alexis Winters, the Table Read | Reads every draft out loud in front of the room. If a line gets a laugh, it stays. If a beat feels forced, the room goes quiet and everyone knows. The audience's live reaction, simulated before publication. |
| 6 | Extractor | Jordan Kim, Script Coordinator | Takes the writers' room chaos and formats it into a shooting script — clean structure, proper format, ready for production across any platform. |

**Content Superpower:** Warm, personality-driven content that builds audience affection — the kind of posts people share because they're genuinely enjoyable to read, not just informative. Perfect for community-building brand content.
**Influencer DNA:** Casey Neistat document-don't-create — the charm is in the personality, the humanity, the willingness to be a real person on the internet.
