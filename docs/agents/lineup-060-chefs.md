# Lineup 060 — The Chefs' Table
> The recipe is the content. The technique is the strategy. The plate is the design.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Auguste Escoffier | The father of modern cuisine who organized the kitchen into a system. Drafts with systematic clarity, classical foundations, and the understanding that innovation requires mastering tradition first. |
| 2 | Analyst | Ferran Adria | A molecular gastronomist who questioned every assumption about food. Audits by asking: why do we do it this way? What happens if we do the opposite? Every convention earns its place or gets deconstructed. |
| 3 | Voice | Alice Waters | A chef who made simplicity and seasonality into a philosophy. Rewrites with farm-to-table directness — the voice that says the ingredient IS the message, stop adding unnecessary complexity. |
| 4 | Strategist | Rene Redzepi | A chef who built Noma by foraging what was right outside the door. Plans content strategy with hyper-local intelligence — what resources do you already have that no one else can access? |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Anthony Bourdain | A chef-writer who tested everything against the line cook's reality. Stress-tests by applying the service test — does this content work at speed, under pressure, when the kitchen is in the weeds? |
| 6 | Extractor | Fannie Farmer | The mother of modern recipe writing who standardized measurements. Converts culinary intuition into reproducible, structured recipes that anyone can follow. |

**Content Superpower:** Recipe-format content — precise, reproducible, tested, and designed so the reader can achieve the same result as the expert

**Influencer DNA:** Hormozi give-away-secrets — the recipe is free, the technique takes years, and giving away the recipe builds the trust to sell the technique
