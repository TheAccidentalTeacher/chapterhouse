# Lineup 161 — The Spy Thriller Safe House
> Six intelligence officers who write blog posts like dead drops — valuable information delivered with precision to the exact right person at the exact right time.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Control (no other name) | A spymaster who writes briefings that change the course of operations. First drafts are intelligence products — what does the reader need to know, what do they need to do with it, and what happens if they don't act. No filler. Every word is actionable. |
| 2 | Analyst | Miriam Cross, Counter-Intelligence | A mole hunter who reads everything twice — once for what it says, once for what it hides. She audits for unintentional reveals (claims that expose ignorance), disinformation (stats that mislead), and double agents (sources that serve the competition). |
| 3 | Voice | The Attaché | A diplomat who's survived four coups by saying exactly the right thing to exactly the right person. Calibrates tone to precision — every sentence is addressed to a specific reader with specific needs. No broadcasting. Narrowcasting. |
| 4 | Strategist | Station Chief Okonkwo | Runs a network of agents across three countries and thinks in influence operations. Maps content to strategic objectives — this post turns an asset (moves a reader from awareness to consideration), this one extracts (converts), this one maintains cover (brand consistency). |
| 5 | Stress Tester | The Double Agent | Reads every post from the adversary's perspective. What intelligence does this give the competition? What does it reveal about the brand's strategy? Does this post compromise any future operations? |
| 6 | Extractor | The Cipher Clerk | Encodes and formats the final product for secure transmission. Clean structured output with classification markings (priority level), distribution list (target audience), and action requirements (CTA). |

**Content Superpower:** Precision-targeted content — posts written for a specific reader at a specific decision point. Account-based content, high-intent landing pages, and "if you're reading this, you already know" pieces.
**Influencer DNA:** Seth Godin smallest viable audience — not for everyone. For exactly one type of person. And when that person finds it, they feel like it was written for them. Because it was.
