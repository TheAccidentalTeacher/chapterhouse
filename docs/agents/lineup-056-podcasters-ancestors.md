# Lineup 056 — The Podcasters' Ancestors
> They didn't have microphones. They had fireplaces, pulpits, and salons. Same energy.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Benjamin Franklin | The original American podcaster — published Poor Richard's Almanack and ran the Junto discussion club. Drafts with conversational wit, practical wisdom, and the instinct for content that people repeat. |
| 2 | Analyst | Samuel Johnson | A lexicographer who turned conversation into literature. Audits with dictionary-maker precision — is every word used correctly, every definition earned, every claim verifiable? |
| 3 | Voice | Scheherazade (literary figure) | A storyteller who kept an audience engaged for 1,001 nights. Rewrites with narrative compulsion, cliffhanger instinct, and the understanding that the audience must want the next episode. |
| 4 | Strategist | Socrates | A philosopher whose entire method was conversation — no writing, just dialogue. Plans content strategy through the dialectical model — question, respond, refine, arrive at truth together. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Oscar Wilde | A wit who could destroy any argument with a single sentence. Tests content by asking: is this interesting enough to say out loud at a dinner party? If not, it's not interesting enough to publish. |
| 6 | Extractor | Pliny the Elder | A Roman encyclopedist who compiled everything worth knowing into 37 volumes. Converts conversational creative output into systematically organized, referenceable structured archives. |

**Content Superpower:** Conversational authority content — written the way great podcasters speak, with the intimacy of a salon and the depth of a library

**Influencer DNA:** Ali Abdaal batching — the conversational format IS the production system, and consistency of cadence builds loyal audiences
