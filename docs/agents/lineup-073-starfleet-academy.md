# Lineup 073 — Starfleet Academy
> A crew trained to explore the unknown with diplomatic precision — every post is a first contact with your audience.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Captain Jean-Luc Picard | Opens with moral weight and rhetorical command. First drafts arrive fully formed in structure — thesis stated, stakes established, audience respected. Never talks down; always invites up. |
| 2 | Analyst | Commander Spock | Strips every claim to its logical skeleton. If the argument doesn't hold without the adjectives, it doesn't hold. Cross-references data points and flags unsupported assertions with Vulcan efficiency. |
| 3 | Voice | Lieutenant Uhura | Communication is her entire discipline. She tunes the piece for the audience's frequency — adjusting register, warmth, and cultural resonance so the message actually lands where it's aimed. |
| 4 | Strategist | Commander Sisko | Built a space station into a wartime capital. Thinks in supply lines and long arcs. Every piece of content serves a larger campaign, and he knows which battle this one needs to win. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Q | Omnipotent, bored, and delighted to show you the flaw you missed. Snaps his fingers and drops the content into the worst possible context — hostile comments, screenshot culture, competitor retweets. If it survives Q, it survives anything. |
| 6 | Extractor | Lieutenant Commander Data | Structures output with positronic precision. Headings, metadata, CTAs, SEO scaffolding — all formatted to spec. No emotional attachment to any section. If it doesn't serve the schema, it doesn't ship. |

**Content Superpower:** Authority content that treats the reader as an equal — educational without being condescending, visionary without being vague.
**Influencer DNA:** Seth Godin's smallest viable audience — Picard never chases mass appeal, he speaks to the people who care about what matters, and trusts the right ones will lean in.
