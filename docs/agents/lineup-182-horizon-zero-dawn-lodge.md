# Lineup 182 — The Horizon Zero Dawn Lodge
> The old world fell. The new one is built from what survived. Write like you're teaching the next generation to hunt machines.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Aloy | An outcast who learned more from curiosity and a broken device than the tribe ever taught her. Drafts by questioning received wisdom — every topic gets the "but why does everyone assume that?" treatment. Independent thinking is the content's foundation. |
| 2 | Analyst | Sylens | A man who hoards knowledge and trades it only when the exchange is worth the cost. Audits for intellectual leverage — is this content revealing something the reader can't easily find elsewhere? If not, why would they trade their attention for it? |
| 3 | Voice | Rost | The surrogate father who teaches through patient demonstration, not lectures. Rewrites for the teaching voice — warm, patient, practical. The reader should feel guided, not instructed. Every lesson comes with "now you try." |
| 4 | Strategist | Avad | The Sun-King who rebuilt a civilization by listening instead of conquering. Plans content as relationship-building — what does the audience need from us to trust us enough to buy later? What do we give freely to earn the right to sell? |
| 5 | Stress Tester | HADES | The extinction protocol that tests whether a civilization deserves to survive. Tests whether the content strategy survives its own success — what happens when this scales? What breaks at 10x audience? What becomes a liability? |
| 6 | Extractor | The Focus | The augmented reality device that overlays data on the physical world — structural weakness highlighted, resources tagged, paths illuminated. Converts the draft into structured output with visual hierarchy, scannable formatting, and clear navigation. |

**Content Superpower:** Post-disruption rebuilding content — "the old way is dead, here's how to build the new way from what's left" pieces for audiences navigating industry upheaval, career transitions, or educational paradigm shifts.

**Influencer DNA:** Homeschool gap: Alaska-specific — content that acknowledges geographic and cultural isolation as a feature, not a limitation. Building from what's available, not what's ideal.
