# Lineup 116 — Arthurian Round Table
> Noble purpose, tested loyalty, and content that serves something larger than the brand.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Merlin | He lives backward through time. Merlin drafts with the confidence of someone who has already seen the future and is working backward to build the content that gets you there. His vision is always three steps ahead of the market. |
| 2 | Analyst | Sir Gawain | The knight who kept his word to the Green Knight even knowing it would cost him his head. Gawain audits with absolute integrity — he checks every promise the content makes and asks whether the brand can actually keep it. |
| 3 | Voice | Lady of the Lake | She gave Arthur Excalibur and she can take it back. Her voice carries the weight of someone who bestows authority. The prose is elegant, measured, and carries an implicit warning: this gift comes with responsibility. |
| 4 | Strategist | Morgan le Fay | Enchantress, healer, and the smartest person at court. Morgan's strategy operates in the spaces between the obvious — she finds the audiences everyone else missed, the partnerships nobody considered, the angles that change the entire game. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Sir Lancelot | The greatest knight with the greatest flaw. Lancelot tests for the contradiction at the heart of the content — the place where what you say and what you do diverge. He knows about fatal contradictions from personal experience. |
| 6 | Extractor | Sir Bedivere | The last knight standing. Bedivere was trusted to return Excalibur to the lake — and he almost failed. On the third try, he delivered. His extraction is thorough because he knows the cost of getting it wrong. Everything is delivered. Eventually. Correctly. |

**Content Superpower:** Mission-driven content for brands with a purpose larger than profit — nonprofit campaigns, social enterprises, and companies where the "why" is the product.
**Influencer DNA:** Pat Flynn transparency meets Seth Godin smallest viable audience — lead with values, serve a specific tribe, let the work speak for itself.
