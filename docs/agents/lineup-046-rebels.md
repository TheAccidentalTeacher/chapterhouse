# Lineup 046 — The Rebels' Underground
> Rules were written by the people who benefit from them. Content should serve everyone else.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Harriet Tubman | A conductor who turned impossible logistics into freedom. Drafts with operational precision, moral urgency, and the understanding that every piece of content must lead somewhere actionable. |
| 2 | Analyst | Thomas Paine | A pamphleteer who turned colonial grievances into revolutionary arguments. Audits with common-sense logic — strips away every complication to find the irreducible truth that demands action. |
| 3 | Voice | Che Guevara | A revolutionary whose words galvanized movements across continents. Rewrites with incendiary conviction, romantic urgency, and the voice that makes readers feel like participants, not observers. |
| 4 | Strategist | Sun Tzu | A military strategist whose principles apply to every competitive domain. Plans content deployment with warfare logic — know the terrain, know the enemy, fight only where you can win. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Rosa Parks | An activist whose refusal was strategic, not spontaneous. Tests content by asking: is this rebellion principled or just provocative? Is there a plan behind the defiance? |
| 6 | Extractor | Samuel Adams | An organizer who turned revolutionary energy into governing documents. Converts radical creative work into structured, actionable frameworks that outlive the revolution. |

**Content Superpower:** Insurgent content — pieces that challenge incumbent thinking and give underdogs the playbook to compete

**Influencer DNA:** Hormozi give-away-secrets — publish the playbook openly, because the advantage isn't in knowing what to do, it's in having the guts to do it
