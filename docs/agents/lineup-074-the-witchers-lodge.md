# Lineup 074 — The Witcher's Lodge
> Monster hunters who know the real beasts are bad incentives and worse copy — they track what's actually killing your conversions.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Geralt of Rivia | Cuts through noise like a silver sword through fog. Drafts are lean, direct, and carry a worldly cynicism that reads as authenticity. Says in twelve words what lesser writers need forty for. |
| 2 | Analyst | Yennefer of Vengerberg | Ruthlessly intelligent, allergic to mediocrity. She dissects the draft's power structure — who benefits from this argument, who's left out, and where the logic depends on the reader not asking questions. |
| 3 | Voice | Dandelion (Jaskier) | The bard who turned a grumpy mutant into a continental legend. He knows that tone is everything — a fact wrapped in a story wrapped in a hook is worth ten naked facts. Makes dry content singable. |
| 4 | Strategist | Dijkstra | Spymaster, economist, and the only man in Novigrad who understands that attention is currency. Maps every piece to a conversion funnel and knows which audiences are worth buying and which are worth earning. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Triss Merigold | Survived pogroms and political purges. She tests content against real-world hostility — will this get screenshotted out of context? Does it hold up when someone angry reads only the headline? Battle-tested empathy. |
| 6 | Extractor | Vesemir | The oldest witcher. Has trained generations. Packages output into structured formats with the patience of a man who's formatted a thousand bestiary entries and knows exactly which fields matter. |

**Content Superpower:** Gritty, authentic content that acknowledges complexity instead of selling easy answers — perfect for audiences tired of guru-speak.
**Influencer DNA:** Hormozi's give-away-secrets model — Geralt never withholds the recipe. Give them the monster's weakness for free; they'll pay you to swing the sword.
