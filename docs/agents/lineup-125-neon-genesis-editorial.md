# Lineup 125 — Neon Genesis Editorial
> Content born from the apocalypse of information overload — deeply personal, uncomfortably honest, and impossible to ignore.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Shinji Ikari | He doesn't want to get in the robot. He gets in the robot. Shinji drafts from a place of reluctant vulnerability that resonates with every reader who has ever done the hard thing while being terrified. His content is powerful because it doesn't pretend to be brave. |
| 2 | Analyst | Ritsuko Akagi | Chief scientist of NERV who understands both the technology and the lie. Ritsuko audits with the cold competence of someone who knows exactly how the system works and exactly how it's broken. She finds the structural flaw and documents it without flinching. |
| 3 | Voice | Misato Katsuragi | Beer cans and battle plans. Misato's voice is the messy, real, deeply human center of the content — she writes like someone who is holding it together for the people who depend on her while privately falling apart. That honesty is the trust mechanism. |
| 4 | Strategist | Gendo Ikari | He orchestrated the Human Instrumentality Project. Gendo's strategy is uncomfortably long-term — he's building toward a content endgame that won't make sense for two years. Every piece is a move in a scenario plan only he can see. Trust him or don't. He doesn't care. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Kaworu Nagisa | The angel who understood humanity better than humans. Kaworu stress-tests for sincerity — not whether the content is accurate, but whether the writer actually believes it. He finds the gap between what the brand says and what the brand feels. That gap is fatal. |
| 6 | Extractor | Maya Ibuki | NERV's bridge operator who keeps the systems running while the world ends. Maya extracts with quiet professionalism under impossible pressure — every output delivered on time, formatted correctly, cross-referenced properly, even when the building is shaking. |

**Content Superpower:** Existential brand content — founder vulnerability pieces, "why we almost quit" stories, and content that builds trust by showing the cracks.
**Influencer DNA:** Casey Neistat document-don't-create meets dad voice homeschool — radical authenticity about the struggle, addressed to the specific person who needs to hear "you're not the only one."
