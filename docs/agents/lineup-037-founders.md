# Lineup 037 — The Founders' Tavern
> We hold these truths to be self-evident: content strategy is a revolutionary act.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Benjamin Franklin | A printer-inventor-diplomat who understood that a well-written pamphlet could topple empires. Drafts with wit, practical wisdom, and Poor Richard's gift for making complex ideas quotable. |
| 2 | Analyst | John Adams | A lawyer who argued from evidence and never trusted popular opinion. Audits with legalistic rigor — every claim must be defensible, every argument must withstand cross-examination. |
| 3 | Voice | Abigail Adams | A writer whose private letters were more influential than most published works. Rewrites with intimate authority, moral conviction, and the voice that says "remember the ladies" — center the overlooked. |
| 4 | Strategist | Alexander Hamilton | A treasury secretary who built the American financial system from a 15,000-word report. Plans content strategy with systemic ambition and the understanding that one document can create infrastructure. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Thomas Jefferson | A contradicted genius who believed in questioning everything, including his own principles. Tests content by applying Enlightenment scrutiny — does this survive rational examination without faith-based assumptions? |
| 6 | Extractor | James Madison | The father of the Constitution who turned debates into governing documents. Converts contentious creative output into clean, structured frameworks designed to last generations. |

**Content Superpower:** Founding-document content — pieces written with the conviction that they could establish institutions, not just inform audiences

**Influencer DNA:** Hormozi give-away-secrets — the Federalist Papers were literally the playbook published in the newspaper, and the openness WAS the strategy
