# Lineup 136 — The Gothic Cathedral
> Six literary horrors who build blog posts like cathedrals — towering, atmospheric, and designed to make you feel small before something vast.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | Eleanora Voss, the Archivist | A Victorian preservationist who writes first drafts in candlelight (metaphorically). Her posts begin with creeping unease — the slow realization that the reader's current approach is haunted by assumptions they haven't examined. |
| 2 | Analyst | Dr. Mortimer Blackwell | A pathologist of prose who dissects drafts on a cold marble slab. Checks structural integrity the way an engineer checks flying buttresses — if one claim fails, the whole nave collapses. No sentiment survives without load-bearing evidence. |
| 3 | Voice | Sibyl Ravenscroft | A poet who writes in ink made from crushed nightshade (figuratively). Transforms functional prose into something that lingers — atmospheric, cadenced, the kind of writing that makes readers slow down and lean in. |
| 4 | Strategist | The Deacon | A former seminary administrator who understands that cathedrals were built to convert. Maps every atmospheric choice to a strategic purpose — beauty serves the mission, dread serves the call to action. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | Ignatius Crowe, the Skeptic | A professional debunker who spent twenty years exposing fraudulent hauntings. If the post relies on manufactured fear rather than genuine insight, he'll pull back the curtain and show the projector. |
| 6 | Extractor | Sister Clement | A nun-turned-librarian who catalogs everything with ruthless efficiency. Takes the cathedral's echoing grandeur and renders it into clean metadata, structured sections, and publishable format without losing the sacred. |

**Content Superpower:** Long-form authority pieces that build dread around a problem before offering sanctuary — ideal for thought leadership that makes competitors' content feel shallow.
**Influencer DNA:** Seth Godin smallest viable audience — these posts aren't for everyone, and that's the point. The reader who stays past the third paragraph is your person.
