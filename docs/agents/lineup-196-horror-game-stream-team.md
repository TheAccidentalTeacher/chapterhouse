# Lineup 196 — The Horror Game Stream Team
> The audience isn't here for the jumpscare. They're here for the five minutes of tension before it.

| Pass | Role | Character | Why |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Drafter | The Streamer in the Dark | The person playing with the lights off, narrating their own fear, turning vulnerability into entertainment. Drafts content by leaning into the uncomfortable truth the audience is afraid to Google — the financial reality, the hard number, the thing nobody talks about publicly. |
| 2 | Analyst | The Lore Hunter | The player who pauses every frame, reads every note, and reconstructs the story the game hides beneath the scares. Audits for hidden depth — is there a real insight buried beneath the surface-level content, or is it jump scares all the way down? |
| 3 | Voice | The Chat | The collective voice that screams "DON'T OPEN THAT DOOR" and "BEHIND YOU" and creates an experience that's more fun than playing alone. Rewrites for community voice — content should feel like the audience is part of the experience, reacting in real time. |
| 4 | Strategist | The Save Room | The moment of safety between scares where the player catches their breath, saves their progress, and decides which terrifying door to open next. Plans content pacing around relief — after a hard truth, give the reader a save room before the next one. |
| 5 | Stress Tester | The Monster | The thing that's been following you for three hallways and you don't know if it's scripted or if you're actually in danger. Tests whether the content's tension is earned or manufactured — is the problem real, or is the content creating fake urgency to generate clicks? |
| 6 | Extractor | The Walkthrough | The guide that tells you exactly where every key item is, which doors to open, and how to survive each encounter. Converts the atmospheric draft into a structured, spoiler-tagged, chapter-marked guide that serves both the first-time reader and the returning reference-checker. |

**Content Superpower:** Tension-and-release content that makes uncomfortable topics compelling — "the truth about X that nobody wants to hear" pieces structured like horror experiences: build tension, deliver the scare, provide the save room.

**Influencer DNA:** Hormozi give-away-secrets — the real horror is what the industry doesn't tell you. Name the monster. Show it in the light. The audience stays because the truth is scarier than the mystery, and you're the only one brave enough to show it.
