What Is Chapterhouse?

Your private operating system for managing all three business tracks.

Chapterhouse is your private operating system — a website only you and Anna can access. It lives at chapterhouse.vercel.app and runs 24/7. Think of it as mission control: you read the morning news, talk to AI, save research, generate content, track tasks, manage dreams, collect intelligence, process email, manage context — all in one place, all tailored to NCHO, SomerSchool, and BibleSaaS.

Nobody else can see it. It's locked to your two email addresses.

Core Concept

Chapterhouse is the command center for all three business tracks (NCHO, SomerSchool, BibleSaaS). Intelligence flows in from multiple sources (daily briefs, email, manual research, Intel reports, YouTube). You decide what matters (via tasks, seeds, opportunities). AI agents automate the rest (digest generation, draft replies, curriculum building, social media).

Quick Stats

  • Screens: 21 total

  • Database: Supabase PostgreSQL (us-west-2)

Chat & Council Mode

Talk to AI. Get advice from five voices at once.

Solo Mode (Default)

Type a question or request in the box at the bottom. Press Enter. Pick which AI to use from the Model dropdown (GPT-5.4, Claude Sonnet 4.6, or Claude Haiku 4.5). The AI reads your daily brief, research, opportunities, and founder memory automatically — so its answers are always in context.

Council Mode

Click the amber Council pill button to toggle it on. Your question goes to five members of the Council of the Unserious:

  • Gandalf (amber) — Creator/architect. Strategic thinking.
  • Lt. Commander Data (blue) — Auditor/analyst. Logical rigor.
  • Polgara (emerald) — Content director. Child-first lens.
  • Earl Harbinger (orange) — Operations commander. Business reality.
  • Beavis & Butthead (zinc) — Engagement stress-test. Is it cool or does it suck?

Each responds in character with colored avatar. After they all weigh in, a rebuttal round follows where they respond to each other.

Special Commands

  • /remember [fact] — Teach the AI something permanently
  • Paste URLs — AI fetches page content automatically
  • Pin conversations — Keep important chats at the top

Daily Brief

Your morning summary. Auto-generated at 7 AM Alaska time.

What You Get

A summary from three sources:

  • 9 RSS news feeds (education, homeschool, edtech)
  • 11 GitHub repos you follow
  • 5 daily.dev feeds (For You, Popular, Anthropic, Security, Next.js)

Reading the Brief

Each item shows headline, why it matters, and track impact (colored dots for NCHO, SomerSchool, BibleSaaS relevance).

⚡ Collisions Section

At the top, items scoring high (≥2) on 2+ business tracks appear with an amber ⚡ badge. These are cross-track opportunities worth paying attention to.

What You Can Do

  • Convert to task — send any item straight to your Tasks list
  • Send to review — put it in Review Queue for later decision
  • Generate new brief — create a fresh one anytime

Intel

Intelligence analysis engine. Feed URLs, get signal from noise.

Three Ways to Add Intel

1. New Session (Manual)

Paste 1–20 URLs. Hit Analyze. System fetches each page, extracts content, runs multi-layer analysis.

2. Publishers Weekly

Paste the raw text of a Publishers Weekly email. Auto-extracts book, market, and trend data.

3. Auto-Fetch

Daily cron at 4 AM Alaska time monitors 5 watch sources (daily.dev, tech news, edtech research, homeschool news). Results auto-inject into Intel.

Reading an Intel Report

  • 🔴 Direct Impact — affects your business immediately
  • 🟡 Ecosystem Signal — market-level trends
  • 🟠 Community Signal — user/customer behavior shifts
  • 🔵 Background — interesting but less urgent

Behind the Scenes

Claude Sonnet 4.6 extracts facts, Claude Haiku 4.5 verifies claims, and the Council provides multi-voice commentary. Results feed into both Dreamer (as proposed seeds) and your chat context (last 48h Intel is always available).

Email Inbox

Auto-sync, auto-categorize, auto-ingest, draft replies.

Two Views

Live View

Raw inbox from connected email accounts (Gmail, Mailcow). Shows all messages as they arrive.

AI View

Categorized and analyzed. Each email shows category (11 types), urgency score (0–5), action_required flag (red pulsing dot), and AI summary.

Auto-Processes (Every 3 Hours)

  1. Sync — Fetch last 30 days, deduplicate
  2. Categorize — Claude Haiku auto-assigns category + urgency + action flag
  3. Auto-ingest — Newsletters → Research. Sales inquiries → Product Intelligence

Draft Reply Button

Click on any email, scroll to bottom, click Draft Reply:

  • Claude Haiku generates a contextual reply
  • You can edit before sending
  • Drafts cache 24 hours
  • Replies route to your email drafts (not auto-sent)

Email Action Banner

On Home page: shows top 5 unread emails that need replies. Click one to jump directly to it in Inbox.

Product Intelligence

AI-powered radar for opportunities.

How to Use It

  1. Click Run Opportunity Analysis (takes ~15 seconds)
  2. AI generates a list of opportunities, each scored on three dimensions:
    • Store score — relevance to your Shopify store
    • Curriculum score — relevance to curriculum products
    • Content score — relevance to content/marketing
  3. Scores range from A+ (very relevant) to C (low relevance)
  4. Expand any opportunity to see supporting evidence and a recommended next action
  5. Mark opportunities as In progress, Done, or Passed

The more research you save, the better these suggestions get.

Context Brain

Four-layer memory system for AI conversations.

Four Slots (Injected in Order)

Slot Document What it Contains
Core copilot-instructions.md Your permanent identity (who you are, business tracks, locked decisions)
Sessions push log + session notes Most recent 8 sessions summarized
Extended extended_context.md Customer research, brand voice rules, copy principles, competitors
Intelligence intel session summaries Last 48 hours of Intel analysis (highest-impact findings)

How to Use It

  1. Click Context Brain in sidebar
  2. Select a document from the pill selector
  3. Read the current content
  4. Edit inline (no external editor needed)
  5. Click Save
  6. Changes auto-inject into every AI call immediately

Push API

For documents outside Chapterhouse, use push tools:

  • PUSH-DREAMER.bat — syncs latest dreamer.md
  • PUSH-ALL.bat — syncs copilot-instructions.md + extended_context.md + dreamer.md

This keeps your workspace (desktop files) and Chapterhouse (cloud context) in sync.

Job Runner

Dashboard for background AI jobs that run while you sleep.

How to Use It

  • Click Create Job to queue a new background AI task
  • Watch progress bars update live as jobs run (powered by Supabase Realtime)
  • Each job shows: label, type badge, status badge, and progress bar
  • Click any job to see full details, output, or error messages
  • Cancel running jobs if needed

Behind the Scenes

Jobs are published to QStash (Upstash), which delivers them to a Railway worker service. The worker processes the AI task, writes progress updates to Supabase, and the UI picks up changes in real time.

Curriculum Factory

5-pass AI pipeline using the Council of the Unserious.

How to Use It

  1. Select a subject, grade level, and duration
  2. Optionally add standards alignment or additional context
  3. Click Generate for single or Batch for many at once

The 5-Pass Process

  1. Gandalf drafts the initial scope & sequence
  2. Lt. Commander Data audits against national standards
  3. Polgara synthesizes into production-ready, child-first version
  4. Earl Harbinger assesses operational viability
  5. Beavis & Butthead stress-test engagement

National Standards Auto-Alignment

Standards auto-detected from subject field. ELA → CCSS-ELA, Math → CCSS-M, Science → NGSS, Social Studies → C3 Framework. No manual input needed.

Batch Mode

Generate up to 70 curricula overnight (10 subjects × 7 grade levels). Jobs are staggered to avoid API rate limits.

Council Chamber

Purpose-built system for generating curriculum scope & sequences as a background job.

How to Use It

  1. Select subject, grade, and duration
  2. Submit to start the job — it runs in the background
  3. Check the Job Runner page for progress
  4. Results appear when complete

How It Differs from Council Mode in Chat

Chat Council Mode: Real-time, general-purpose, handles any topic.

Council Chamber: Purpose-built for curriculum generation, runs as a background job.

Your Daily Workflow

How a typical day uses all of Chapterhouse.

Morning (7 AM)

  • Daily brief auto-generates
  • Email inbox auto-syncs and categorizes overnight
  • Read the brief, especially ⚡ Collisions section
  • Check Email Action Banner for top 5 emails needing replies
  • Read any Intel reports from 4 AM auto-fetch

Breakfast Review

  • Open Dreamer — check Earl's AI suggestions
  • Decide: which seeds should become Active? Which dismissed?
  • Check Context Brain — update founder memory if needed

During the Day

  • Quick questions: Use Chat (toggle Council Mode on/off)
  • Save discoveries: Use Research to paste articles, screenshots, notes
  • Reply to mail: Use Email Inbox → Draft Reply on any inquiry
  • Deep brainstorming: Use Council Mode for complex decisions

Afternoon

  • Open Context Brain, make edits to keep founder memory fresh
  • Content creation: Use Content Studio or YouTube Intelligence

End of Day

  • Check Tasks — anything blocked? Anything done?
  • Check Review Queue — any pending decisions?
  • Check Dreamer — any seeds ready to promote?
  • Tomorrow's brief will auto-generate at 7 AM

Collision Scoring

Every daily brief item is scored 0–3 on three axes (ncho, somersschool, biblesaas). Items scoring ≥2 on 2+ tracks become "⚡ Collisions" flagged at top of brief. These are cross-track opportunities.

Known Limitations & Workarounds

Constraints and how to work around them.

Limitation Workaround
Document editing — Read-only in UI Edit source files, use Push API to sync
YouTube transcripts — YouTube blocks cloud IPs System auto-falls back to Gemini 2.5 Flash on Railway (~77 sec) — works 100%
Email sync latency — Every 3 hours Check Gmail/Mailcow directly for real-time
Some RSS feeds — 3 of 9 blocked Brief generates from working feeds; coverage varies
n8n Pipelines — Requires API key Add N8N_API_KEY and N8N_BASE_URL to Vercel environment
Draft replies — Cache 24 hours Click Draft Reply again to regenerate if stale
Context size — ~200KB injected per call Most work fine; large documents may hit limits on edge cases
Bell icon — No live notifications Check Home page → Email Action Banner for priority emails
