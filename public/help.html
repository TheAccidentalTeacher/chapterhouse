Chapterhouse Help Guide

Plain-English guide to everything the app does, how to use it, and what to expect.

What Is Chapterhouse?

Chapterhouse is your private operating system — a website only you and Anna can access. It lives at chapterhouse.vercel.app and runs 24/7. Think of it as mission control: you read the morning news, talk to AI, save research, generate content, track tasks, manage dreams, collect intelligence, process email, manage context — all in one place, all tailored to Next Chapter and SomerSchool.

Nobody else can see it. It's locked to your two email addresses.

Core concept: Chapterhouse is the command center for all three business tracks (NCHO, SomerSchool, BibleSaaS). Intelligence flows in from multiple sources (daily briefs, email, manual research, Intel reports, YouTube). You decide what matters (via tasks, seeds, opportunities). AI agents automate the rest (digest generation, draft replies, curriculum building, social media).

Quick Stats

  • Screens: 21 total (organized into 6 sidebar groups)
  • AI Models: Claude Sonnet 4.6, GPT-5.4, Claude Haiku 4.5, Gemini 2.5 Flash
  • Database: Supabase (PostgreSQL, us-west-2)
  • Hosting: Vercel Pro + Railway background workers
  • Monthly Cost: ~$15–25 Vercel + ~$50–100 AI API usage

Quick Start

Get up and running in 5 minutes.

First Time Here?

  1. Log in with scott@nextchapterhomeschool.com or anna@nextchapterhomeschool.com
  2. Go to Home (sidebar, left side). You'll see a chat input box.
  3. Read your daily brief (auto-generated at 7 AM Alaska time, even on your first day)
  4. Check your Email Action Banner on the Home page for any emails needing replies
  5. Explore the sidebar — 6 groups with 21 screens total

The Six Groups

Group What It Does

Dream & Intelligence Manage ideas (Dreamer), track market signals (Intel), process email
Intelligence & Research Save research, spot opportunities, extract curriculum from YouTube

Chat & Council Mode

Talk to AI. Get advice from the Council of the Unserious.

Chat Basics

Type a question or request in the box at the bottom. Press Enter. One AI responds — or toggle Council Mode to get five voices at once.

Solo Mode (Default)

Pick which AI to use from the Model dropdown (GPT-5.4, Claude Sonnet 4.6, or Claude Haiku 4.5). Ask anything. The AI reads your daily brief, research, opportunities, and founder memory automatically — so its answers are always in context.

Council Mode

Click the amber Council pill button to toggle it on. Your question goes to five members of the Council of the Unserious:

  • Gandalf (amber) — Creator/architect. Strategic thinking.
  • Lt. Commander Data (blue) — Auditor/analyst. Logical rigor.
  • Polgara (emerald) — Content director. Child-first lens.
  • Earl Harbinger (orange) — Operations commander. Business reality.
  • Beavis & Butthead (zinc) — Engagement stress-test. Is it cool or does it suck?

Each responds in character with colored avatar. After they all weigh in, a rebuttal round follows where they respond to each other. It's like having a board meeting in your chat.

Advanced Chat Features

  • /remember [fact] — Teach the AI something permanently. Example: /remember We decided to launch SomerSchool by August
  • Paste URLs — The AI fetches page content automatically (up to 12K chars)
  • Auto-learning — The system extracts key facts from your conversation and saves them to founder memory
  • Pin conversations — Keep important chats at the top of the thread list

Daily Brief

Your morning summary. Auto-generated every day at 7 AM Alaska time.

What You Get

A summary of overnight developments across:

  • 9 RSS news feeds (education, homeschool, edtech)
  • 11 GitHub repos you follow
  • 5 daily.dev feeds (For You, Popular, Anthropic, Security, Next.js)

Reading the Brief

Each item shows:

  • Headline — What happened
  • Why it matters — How it connects to your work
  • Track impact — Colored dots showing relevance: NCHO (store), SomerSchool (curriculum), BibleSaaS (Bible study)

⚡ Collisions Section

At the top, items that score high (≥2) on 2+ business tracks appear in the "Collisions" section with an amber ⚡ badge. These are cross-track opportunities worth paying attention to.

Example collision: "Penguin Random House launches Christian curriculum platform" → Direct for NCHO (positioning opportunity), high relevance to SomerSchool market validation, medium for BibleSaaS audience.

What You Can Do

  • Convert to task — Send any item straight to your Tasks list
  • Send to review — Put it in Review Queue for later decision
  • Generate new brief — Click the button to create a fresh one anytime

Dreamer

Your idea management system. Seeds → Active → Building → Shipped.

The Kanban Board

Dreamer is a kanban board with four columns:

Column What Goes Here
Seeds Raw ideas (from chat, Intel, daily brief, manual entry)
Active Ideas you're seriously considering
Building Projects currently in progress
Shipped Completed and live

Using Dreamer

  • Drag cards between columns to change status
  • Add manually — Click + to create a new seed
  • View source — Each card shows where it came from (dreamer.md, chat, brief, Intel, push API)
  • Timestamps — Track when each idea was created or moved
  • Archive/Dismiss — Hide ideas you've decided against

Earl's AI Review

Click Get AI Review and Earl Harbinger (Claude Sonnet 4.6) analyzes all your seeds and suggests:

  • Promote — High-impact, prioritize it
  • Dismiss — Not worth pursuing
  • Hold — Keep warm but not now
  • Merge — Combine with another seed

You always approve manually — Earl advises, you decide.

Daily Dream Log

Each day, you can add one mood check-in to track energy and focus. View the log over time to spot patterns.

Intel

Intelligence analysis engine. Feed URLs, get signal from noise.

Three Ways to Add Intel

1. New Session (Manual)

Paste 1–20 URLs. Hit Analyze. System fetches each page, extracts content, runs multi-layer analysis.

2. Publishers Weekly

Paste the raw text of a Publishers Weekly email. Auto-extracts book, market, and trend data.

3. Auto-Fetch

Daily cron at 4 AM Alaska time monitors 5 watch sources (daily.dev, tech news, edtech research, homeschool news). Results auto-inject into Intel.

Reading an Intel Report

Each report shows:

  • Summary — Top findings from all URLs
  • Categorized sections:
    • 🔴 Direct Impact (affects your business immediately)
    • 🟡 Ecosystem Signal (market-level trends)
    • 🟠 Community Signal (user/customer behavior shifts)
    • 🔵 Background (interesting but less urgent)
  • Impact scores: A+ (act immediately) → C (background)
  • Affected repos — Which of your 47 repos this matters for
  • Proposed seeds — AI auto-suggests 1–3 seed ideas to add to Dreamer

Behind the Scenes

Claude Sonnet 4.6 extracts facts, Claude Haiku 4.5 verifies claims, and the Council provides multi-voice commentary. Results feed into both Dreamer (as proposed seeds) and your chat context (last 48h Intel always available).

Email Inbox

Auto-sync, auto-categorize, auto-ingest, draft replies.

Two Views

Live View

Raw inbox from connected email accounts (Gmail, ncho@mailcow). Shows all messages as they arrive.

AI View

Categorized and analyzed. Each email shows:

  • Category (11 types: spam, vendor, sales, customer, newsletter, etc.)
  • Urgency score (0–5)
  • Action required flag (red pulsing dot)
  • AI summary of content

Auto-Processes (Every 3 Hours)

  1. Sync — Fetch last 30 days from Gmail/Mailcow, deduplicate
  2. Categorize — Claude Haiku auto-assigns category + urgency + action flag
  3. Auto-ingest — Newsletters → Research queue. Sales/customer → Product Intelligence

Draft Reply Button

Click on any email, scroll to bottom, click Draft Reply (orange button):

  • Claude Haiku generates a contextual reply
  • You can edit before sending
  • Drafts cache 24 hours (regenerate if stale)
  • Replies route to your email drafts (not auto-sent)

Email Action Banner

On the Home page: shows top 5 unread emails needing replies. Click to jump directly to Inbox.

Cost

~$0.001 per draft reply (Haiku). Auto-ingest + digest + categorization bundled into overall brief cost (~$0.05/day).

Context Brain

Your four-layer memory system.

What It Is

Context Brain manages all documents that feed into AI conversations. These documents are automatically injected into every chat call, every brief generation, and every Council Chamber session.

Four Slots (Injected in Order)

Slot Document Contains
Core copilot-instructions.md Your permanent identity (who you are, business tracks, all locked decisions)
Sessions push log + session notes Most recent 8 sessions summarized (so AI knows what you decided)
Extended extended_context.md Customer research, brand voice rules, copy principles, competitors
Intel intel summaries Last 48 hours of Intel analysis (highest-impact findings)

How to Use It

  1. Click Context Brain in sidebar
  2. Select a document from the pill selector
  3. Read the current content
  4. Edit inline (no external editor needed)
  5. Click Save
  6. Changes auto-inject into every AI call immediately

Push API

For documents outside Chapterhouse (in your desktop workspace), use push tools:

  • PUSH-DREAMER.bat — syncs latest dreamer.md
  • PUSH-ALL.bat — syncs copilot-instructions.md + extended_context.md + dreamer.md

This keeps your workspace (desktop files) and Chapterhouse (cloud context) in sync automatically.

Collision Scoring

Every daily brief item is scored 0–3 on three axes:

  • ncho — relevance to homeschool store
  • somersschool — relevance to course platform
  • biblesaas — relevance to Bible study app

Items scoring ≥2 on 2+ tracks become "⚡ Collisions" flagged at top of brief. These are cross-track opportunities worth attention.

Example collision: "Penguin Random House launches Christian curriculum DTC platform" → Direct for NCHO (positioning + partnership opportunity), high for SomerSchool (validates market), medium for BibleSaaS (different audience but same market segment).

FAQ & Quick Reference

Common questions answered.

Access & Security

Who can access Chapterhouse?

Only scott@nextchapterhomeschool.com and anna@nextchapterhomeschool.com (or your personal Gmail accounts configured in the system).

Is my data private?

Yes — stored in your private Supabase (PostgreSQL) database in us-west-2. No one else has access. Never shared with third parties.

How do I sign out?

Click the door icon in the top right corner of the page header.

AI & Costs

What AI models are used?

  • Chat: GPT-5.4 (solo) or Claude Sonnet 4.6 (trio roles)
  • Council: Gandalf + Data + Polgara (Claude Sonnet), Earl (GPT-5.4), Beavis & Butthead (GPT-5-mini)
  • Brief: Claude Haiku 4.5 (collision scoring)
  • Email: Haiku 4.5 (categorize, draft replies), Sonnet 4.6 (digest)
  • Social: Claude Sonnet 4.6
  • YouTube: Gemini 2.5 Flash (transcripts)
  • Curriculum: All Council members + Claude Sonnet 4.6 (extraction)

How much does it cost?

~$15–25 Vercel Pro + ~$50–100 AI API usage monthly depending on usage volume.

Automation & Timing

When do things auto-run?

  • Daily brief: 7 AM Alaska time, every day
  • Email sync: Every 3 hours
  • Email categorize: Every 3 hours
  • Intel fetch: 4 AM Alaska time
  • Email digest: 12:30 AM (00:30 UTC)
  • Social batch posts: Monday 5 AM Alaska time (12 PM UTC)

Memory & Teaching

How do I teach Chapterhouse something?

Three ways:

  1. Type /remember [fact] in Chat
  2. Go to Settings → Founder Memory → Add
  3. Use Context Brain to edit copilot-instructions.md directly

How long does it remember?

Permanently. Facts saved to Founder Memory or Context Brain stay until you delete them.

Troubleshooting

Something's broken. What do I do?

  1. Check Settings → Environment Status for red/yellow indicators
  2. Most issues are API key misconfiguration
  3. Check Vercel logs at vercel.com/dashboard
  4. Email scott@nextchapterhomeschool.com for support

Why are some feeds showing no new items?

About 3 of 9 daily brief feeds are blocked by source websites (they detect Chapterhouse is a bot). The brief still generates — it just has fewer sources some days.

Why did my YouTube transcript take 77 seconds?

YouTube blocks cloud IPs, so Chapterhouse falls back to Gemini 2.5 Flash on Railway. It watches the actual video and transcribes it. 77 seconds for a 20-minute video is normal.
